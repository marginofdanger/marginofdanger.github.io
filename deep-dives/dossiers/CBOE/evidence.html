Evidence

Claims by section
business-model
  • ~half of gross revenue is a pass-through Cboe never keeps, so net revenue ($2,429M FY2025) is the real top line, and ~63% of it, plus the bulk of operating income, sits in one segment, Options, behind one exclusive license.
  • a 14x lower RPC for the same matching engine
  • From roughly 27% of the segment's contract volume (index ADV 4.9M of 18.4M Cboe-touched), index options generate about 84% of the segment's net transaction fees
  • On index options Cboe is a price-setter because no rival venue can list SPX; the only discipline is the substitute (SPY options, prediction-market binaries) and keeping the liquidity ecosystem intact, not a competing exchange.
  • Zero-days-to-expiry (0-DTE) SPX options went from ~43% of SPX volume in Q4 2022 to over 61% in Q4 2025; index contract ADV rose from 1.9M (2019) to 4.9M (2025) and 6.1M in Q1 2026, a roughly 25% compound rate on a 43-year-old product.
  • That produces extreme operating leverage on index-driven growth: the Options segment converted 86% of incremental net revenue to operating income FY2024->FY2025 and 88% in Q1 2026 (versus a soft 30% in the flat-volume FY2023->FY2024 year).
  • One customer was ~10% of total company revenue in FY2025 ($480.0M, up from $403.1M in FY2024 and $389.4M in FY2023 — concentration rising in absolute dollars), a large market-maker/order-flow relationship spread across all five segments, with no contractual lock-in
  • three clearing members clear ~71% of the market-maker sides at all of Cboe's U.S. options exchanges, and ~69% of net transaction-and-clearing fees route through OCC
  • The entire U.S.-plus-Canada equities trading book nets only ~$124M; the other ~$274M of the segment's $407M net revenue is a recurring access-and-data annuity (access $144.4M + market data $129.7M) that flows to net almost dollar-for-dollar. The segment therefore earns a 46.2% operating margin on net revenue (59.8% in Q1 2026) despite the "thin net-capture" label
  • It is structurally mature: FY2025 ADV (227k) sits below 2016 (239k) and well below the 2017 peak (295k).
  • It is the highest-incremental-margin grower in the portfolio - operating income rose 17x ($2.7M to $46.0M) over FY2021-FY2025 on revenue up ~1.6x, because operating expenses were flat in absolute dollars.
  • The same Cboe Titanium engine yields $0.924 per index contract and $0.066 per multi-listed contract. The difference is entirely the exclusive license plus the liquidity network that has accreted around SPX/VIX for 30-plus years.
  • Data Vantage is ~26% of net revenue, ~98% gross margin, and is spread across all five segments.
capital-allocation
  • Cboe is a capital-light allocator: 10-year cumulative capex was only $488M against ~$8.4bn of reported operating cash flow (capex ~6% of OCF, ~3% of net revenue), so almost all cash is available for M&A, dividends, and buybacks.
  • Size growth reached the per-share line: operating income per share compounded ~19% and net revenue per share ~12% over FY2017-FY2025, because the share count declined (the inverse of dilutive expansion). The bulk of per-share growth is organic operating leverage — operating margin on net revenue expanded from 37.3% (FY2017) to 60.4% (FY2025) — not financial engineering.
capitalization
  • Cboe runs a deliberately under-levered, all-fixed-rate balance sheet and is now net cash. Total debt is $1,442.9M (carrying; $1,450.0M principal) in three tranches of unsecured senior notes, against $2,216.5M cash and $36.1M financial investments — roughly $0.8bn net cash. Interest coverage is ~32x against a 4.0x covenant floor.
competitive-advantage
  • That franchise throws off a ~73% segment operating margin and a 14x revenue-per-contract premium over the commodity multi-listed book on the same matching engine, and the premium is widening, not eroding — index RPC rose every year from $0.832 (2021) to $0.940 (Q1 2026) while index volume compounded ~25% annually.
  • This is a genuine liquidity-network moat with a contractual cliff: the crown-jewel exclusivity runs only through 2032, and rivals are already attacking the benchmark.
  • The advantage is best described as two businesses: a wide-moat index franchise that holds essentially all the economic rent, wrapped around a no-moat multi-listed utility that is a price-taker losing share.
  • MIAX signed a 10-year exclusive license with Bloomberg in July 2024 to list cash-settled options on the Bloomberg 500 Index, a deliberate S&P-500-proxy competitor (the SEC approved cash-settled B500 options in May 2025).
  • CME's analogous S&P license is architecturally stronger because it is tied to CME's equity ownership of the S&P DJI joint venture and stays exclusive as long as CME owns at least 5% of the JV — effectively perpetual — whereas Cboe's is a fixed-term lease.
  • ROIC rose from ~10–13% (FY2018–2021, weighed down by Bats goodwill in invested capital) to 22.4% in FY2025, durably above any reasonable 8–9% cost of capital.
  • CBOE's consolidated operating margin (60.4%) sits below CME (64.9%) but well above ICE and Nasdaq, and its Options segment margin (72.7%) is above every peer's consolidated margin — including CME, itself the highest-margin peer because it is a near-pure derivatives business.
competitive-landscape
  • Cboe is the uncontested scale leader where it owns the product (index options, VIX futures, pan-European equity clearing), a slowly bleeding incumbent where the product is a commodity (U.S. multi-listed options, U.S. cash equities), and a mid-pack growth player in FX.
  • proprietary index options (an effective monopoly — Cboe held ~98.7% index-options share in FY2021 and ~100% since, because SPX is single-listed)
  • MIAX (~18%) and MEMX (~17% non-penny) are closing the gap fastest
  • Cboe is the #3 U.S. equities operator behind NYSE Group and Nasdaq, and the clear share loser of the three: its U.S.-equities exchange share has declined every year from 15.8% (FY2020) to 10.0% (FY2025) to 9.8% (Q1 2026), roughly a third of its share gone in six years.
  • The dominant structural force is off-exchange internalization, which crossed 50% of all U.S. equity volume for the first time in November 2024 and now runs ~52.4% — so the on-exchange pool all three exchanges fight over is a shrinking minority of total volume.
  • Within listed futures Cboe is a rounding error — 227k contracts/day is ~0.8% of CME's 28.1M and ~0.6% of the CME-plus-ICE complex — but in equity-volatility futures it is an uncontested monopolist, because no licensed competitor exists and CME's own CVOL volatility suite explicitly excludes S&P 500 equity volatility.
  • Cboe Europe is the single largest pan-European equities venue at ~25.0% share (FY2025), gaining (24.0% to 25.0% over 2023-2025) while net capture rose (€0.226 to €0.269 bps) — rising price and rising share, the opposite of commodity fee compression.
  • Cboe Clear Europe is the largest pan-European cash-equity CCP at ~39.4% cleared share, rising (34.9% to 39.4% over 2023-2025, then 40.2% in Q1 2026), and it out-clears its own trading share because its interoperable model lets it clear for venues it does not own - a structural edge the vertical-silo incumbents (Euronext Clearing, Eurex Clearing) cannot copy without cannibalizing their captive venues.
  • Cboe FX is a mid-pack electronic spot-FX venue (~$50bn/day spot ADV, ~4th-5th among public-data venues, behind LSEG ~$106bn, FXSpotStream ~$77bn, and CME's EBS ~$64bn). It is sub-scale relative to the ~$2.1tn/day global spot-FX market it sells into (~2.4% share). But it grew spot ADV faster than the two scale leaders in 2025 (+9.5% vs LSEG +6.1% and EBS +7.4%).
  • Total options market share slipped to 29.1% (Q1 2026) and multi-listed share to 22.3%, which looks like competitive loss. The opposite is true economically: Cboe is ceding low-value multi-listed volume (net $0.066/contract) to MIAX and MEMX while gaining high-value index volume (net $0.924/contract) where it has ~100% share and no share to lose.
expectations
  • Management's standing through-cycle framework (mid-single-digit organic) is the conservative class-median; consensus and management's just-raised 2026 guide sit at the top of the realized distribution, reachable only if elevated volatility persists.
m-and-a
  • The core deals worked: Bats (2017, $3,960M) built the modern company and beat its synergy target, and its goodwill has never been impaired; the European clearing tuck-ins (EuroCCP/Cboe Clear Europe, which even produced a $32.6M bargain-purchase gain) held their value. The digital-asset bet failed fast and completely: ErisX/Cboe Digital (~$564M, May 2022) had ~96% of its purchase price written off within 2-3 years - the entire $460.9M of goodwill impaired the same year it closed, plus an $81.0M intangible impairment in 2024.
  • The 2025-26 realignment is now reversing the 2020-2022 international tuck-ins (Japan wound down, Canada/Australia sold to TMX, CEDX wound down), which management quantifies as ~3% of net revenue and ~8-10% of opex - i.e. the units being exited were roughly breakeven-to-loss-making, a tacit admission several international deals did not earn their keep even where they produced no headline writedown.
risks
  • Exposure: ~68% of net revenue is the Options-plus-Futures segments, "the majority of which" is exclusively-licensed/proprietary index product; the 14x index-to-multi-listed RPC gap is the entire economic value of exclusivity.
secular-trends
  • same-day SPX trading went from over 43% of SPX volume in Q4 2022 to over 61% in Q4 2025; index contract ADV rose from 1.9M (2019) to 4.9M (2025) and 6.1M in Q1 2026. The inflection point was clearly 2022 (+44% index ADV), the year Cboe added Tuesday/Thursday and ultimately full Monday-Friday SPX expirations.
  • global-trading-hours SPX volume grew over 30%, 7 of 10 identified Korean local brokers now offer SPX options (versus 0 online two years ago), and Robinhood onboarded.
  • Cboe is a modest share loser in the contestable multi-listed pool, not a share winner in options broadly -- the revenue growth is a product-mix shift toward a proprietary line, not competitive share capture.
  • The cleanest cyclical tell is Futures ADV, which tracks volatility most directly: it declined -5% for FY2025 (227.2k versus 238.6k) before rebounding +14% in Q1 2026 -- direct evidence the cyclical layer oscillates.
technology
  • The clearest proof is that the same Cboe Titanium engine produces a 14x-higher RPC on the exclusive product than on the commodity product.
  • SPX options are cash-settled options on the S&P 500, listed exclusively on Cboe under a license from S&P Dow Jones Indices that runs through December 31, 2033, with exclusive rights to trade S&P 500 index options through December 31, 2032.
  • management says incremental revenue delivered onto the platform "approaches nearly 100%" margin, and Cboe integrated nine acquisitions onto it in five years with 100% uptime through 2020-2021.
  • Data Vantage net revenue grew 19% organically year-over-year in Q1 2026, but ~85-90% of that growth came from new units and new sales rather than price increases
  • 58% of SPX notional value still trades on the floor (versus 16% of contracts) because complex multi-leg institutional risk "can't be replicated electronically" — a genuine switching-cost barrier for the highest-value flow, not a relic.
value-chain
  • Options is 51.6% of gross revenue but 63.0% of net, because the proprietary index piece has almost no rebate or royalty drag; North American Equities is 35.5% of gross but only 16.8% of net, because the rebate eats most of it.
  • For every $1 of net derivatives revenue Cboe keeps, it pays S&P/index licensors about $0.15 in royalty ($201.8M Derivatives royalty against $1,341M Derivatives net revenue). S&P then earns a 69%-operating-margin business on that royalty stream — the licensor sits above Cboe in profitability on the SPX franchise.
  • (1) the S&P license window (exclusive SPX rights through Dec 31, 2032; full license through 2033), which the 10-K calls out as the single most material dependency; (2) VIX methodology exclusivity, which depends on continued access to SPX option data, which depends on (1); (3) sole-clearer dependence at OCC for all U.S. options and VIX futures; and (4) clearing-member concentration.
