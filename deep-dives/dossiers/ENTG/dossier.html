1. Business description, model & unit economics

Entegris supplies critical advanced materials and process solutions to semiconductor makers and other high-technology manufacturers (FY2025 10-K, Item 1). It does two economically distinct things. It sells advanced materials and chemistries that go onto the wafer — deposition precursors, CMP slurries and pads, etch and clean chemistries, ion-implant gases. And it sells filtration, purification, and contamination-control products that protect the purity of the liquids, gases, wafers, and reticles used throughout a fab. FY2025 net sales were $3,196.6M, down from $3,241.2M in FY2024 and $3,523.9M in FY2023, with the two-year decline mostly divestitures rather than lost demand. About 75% of revenue is unit-driven — consumables consumed as wafers are made — so it tracks wafer volumes more than the more cyclical capital-equipment cycle, and about 25% is capex-driven (FOUPs and fluid-handling hardware tied to fab construction and wafer-fab-equipment spend)❝ (Q1 2026 call).

Revenue is 82% international, with Taiwan the single largest geography at ~23% and China at ~21%. TSMC is the only named customer, at 16% of net sales in FY2025 (up from 11% in FY2023); the top-10 customers reached 50% (up from 43% in FY2023). No single product platform exceeds 3% of net sales across tens of thousands of SKUs, so the portfolio is deep but diffuse (FY2025 10-K, Item 1).

What sets price: Products are qualified — held as "positions of record" — inside a customer's process recipe at a specific node. Switching away from a qualified filter, slurry, or precursor risks yield on wafers worth far more than the consumable, so the buyer optimizes for defectivity and supply assurance rather than unit price.❝ The 10-K frames the basis of competition as "technical expertise, time to solution, product quality and performance… total cost of ownership," with price "an important factor" but not the first (FY2025 10-K, Item 1). The mechanism is a qualification switching cost, and it shows up as durable share and content growth, not as pricing power — the departing CEO stated realized pricing "has been essentially flat for us over the last several years… A lot of our growth really comes from volumes" (Bernstein conference, May 2025). Contracts are short: supply agreements "typically do not include long-term purchase commitments," most do not exceed twelve months, and consolidated contract liabilities were only $49.9M at year-end, so there is no backlog annuity❝ — durability comes from spec-in and the consumable replacement cycle, not from contracted volume (FY2025 10-K, Note 2; Item 1).

What drives volume: Two forces: wafer starts (the market silicon index, MSI) and content per wafer. As nodes shrink and 3D structures grow taller, each wafer consumes more process steps and more purity control, so served content per wafer rises. Management sizes the long-run algorithm as industry units at roughly twice GDP plus 3-6 points of content-driven outgrowth❝ (Investor Update, September 2022). That outgrowth was real pre-2022 but has not been visible at the consolidated level for three years: FY2025 unit-driven revenue grew ~2%, which management itself called "in line with wafer starts," i.e. zero consolidated outgrowth, while capex-driven revenue declined 7% with fab-construction spending (Q4 2025 call).

The demand-driver tree makes the exposure explicit, because "correlated to MSI" is a proxy, not the ultimate driver. MSI is ~90% mainstream/mature wafers and only ~10% leading-edge, so the ~75%-unit-driven engine is a trailing-edge volume bet — even though ~60% of revenue is advanced logic and memory, because content per wafer is ~2x higher at the leading edge and over-weights the revenue book toward it. ENTG's volume therefore tracks the mature-node cycle, which is why the 2023-2025 stall was the trailing-edge post-COVID inventory correction, not something specific to ENTG.

Per-family gross margin and per-unit input cost cannot be built below the segment line — Note 20 gives cost of sales only for the combined segment, and the raw inputs (filtration membranes, polymer resins) are named as categories, several sole/single/limited-source, but not sized [Tier-3 gap] (FY2025 10-K, Item 1). At the segment level FY2025 reconciles: cost of sales $995.1M on sales $1,799.1M is a 44.7% gross margin, operating expense $377.6M, segment profit $426.4M.

The consumable/hardware split explained FY2025 directly: APS sales declined 3% "mainly due to a decline in facilities-based capital expenditure investments," which cut demand for fluid-handling products and FOUPs, "partially offset by an increase in sales from gas and liquid filtration products❝" (FY2025 10-K, MD&A). The consumable half grew while the capex half declined. Q1 2026 shows the recovery mirror image: liquid filtration posted a third consecutive record quarter and FOUPs a three-year revenue high (Q1 2026 call). APS is the more capital-intensive of the two segments on the growth-capex margin — its depreciation grew 49% over FY2023-FY2025, from $77.3M (4.2% of sales) to $115.5M (6.4%), as the Kaohsiung (Taiwan) and Colorado Springs plants landed ahead of the volume, which is the single largest identifiable driver of the margin compression.❝

2. Key technologies & technical differentiators

Entegris is not a single-technology moat like CUDA. It is a portfolio of tens of thousands of small, specified-in consumable products, where a "large product platform" is only ~$50M of revenue and no platform exceeds 3% of sales.❝ The real technical differentiation is concentrated in a few places, and the overarching mechanism across all of them is the qualification switching cost — a fab will not re-qualify a filter or slurry mid-node for a small price delta because a yield excursion costs far more than the consumable. R&D backs it: engineering, research and development ran $329.0M in FY2025 (10.3% of sales, up from 7.9% in FY2023), with ~1,600 R&D staff and ~4,400 active patents, though the 10-K states it does "not consider any particular Company patent… to be material," so the moat is diffuse trade-secret and qualification know-how rather than a blocking-patent wall (FY2025 10-K, Item 1).

Ion-implant gas delivery (SDS/VAC): Ion implantation dopes wafers with hazardous gases (arsine, phosphine, boron trifluoride). Traditional cylinders store these above atmospheric pressure, so any leak drives toxic gas out. Entegris's Safe Delivery Source stores the gas adsorbed onto a proprietary carbon adsorbent at sub-atmospheric pressure, so a leak draws air in rather than pushing gas out — a step-change in safety — and packs more gas per cylinder. The moat is a proprietary adsorbent, a safety-qualification lock-in (a fab will not swap the system storing arsine in its cleanroom for a marginal saving), and an installed base of implant tools engineered around SDS.❝ It has led the niche for 20+ years and was "adopted by the entire industry" (entegris.com, accessed 2026-07-07) [web]. This is a cash-cow annuity, not a forward bet.

The molybdenum system, the crown-jewel roadmap bet: As word lines and contacts shrink, tungsten's resistivity climbs and it needs a titanium-nitride barrier liner that eats the shrinking space. Molybdenum has lower effective resistivity at nanoscale and essentially no diffusivity into the dielectric, so it can be deposited without a barrier, saving steps and reclaiming space, which matters most in high-layer-count 3D NAND. The differentiation is not the metal but that moly's solid precursors are "notoriously difficult to handle and deliver." Entegris built a cross-segment system — a ProE-Vap sublimation canister that turns the solid into gas, corrosion-resistant coatings for the gas lines, gas purification, and delivery cabinets — which a point competitor cannot easily match❝ (Q1 2024 call; Bernstein, May 2025). External validation: Lam Research launched ALTUS Halo (February 2025), the first ALD tool for molybdenum in high-volume manufacturing, cutting resistance more than 50% versus tungsten, with early adoption at Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron (newsroom.lamresearch.com, accessed 2026-07-07) [web]. The content is incremental — "we do not make the deposition materials Moly replaces" — so it is new served market, not cannibalization (Q3 2024 call). This is the single largest unshipped-technology dependency in the forward case; if moly positions-of-record disappoint or push out a node, the content-per-wafer algorithm slows.❝ The competing next-metal candidate is ruthenium, and no workpaper established whether ENTG holds the ruthenium precursor position — an open question flagged in Section 20.

Liquid microcontamination filtration: Photoresist, ultra-pure water, and aggressive chemistries must be filtered to remove nanometer particles and dissolved metals before reaching the wafer; at 2nm a single killer defect scraps die. Membrane casting and modification is a materials-science discipline, filters are qualified per fab process, and recent introductions are "currently being utilized in 2 nanometer node production" (FY2025 10-K, Item 1). This is ENTG's most consistently growing line, with a third consecutive record quarter in Q1 2026. But it is a #2 position: third-party reports put Pall (Danaher) at ~35% of the semiconductor liquid-filter market versus Entegris ~28%❝, Pall invested ~$150M in a Singapore filter plant in 2024, and Chinese entrant Cobetter is named as a competitor (market.us, accessed 2026-07-07) [web, low-med]. It is a strong duopoly-plus position with a well-funded #1 rival and a rising China threat, not a monopoly.

Microenvironment (FOUPs, EUV pods): FOUPs are sealed high-purity carriers for 300mm wafers; EUV reticle pods protect pellicle-sensitive masks. The 10-K states "We lead the market for 300 millimeter FOUPs," corroborated at ~28% of a ~$730M market [web]. But two firms claim FOUP/FOSB leadership (Shin-Etsu Polymer co-claims a large share), and the fastest-growing adjacency — EUV/reticle pods — is led by ASML-certified, Taiwan-listed Gudeng, not Entegris.❝ This leg is a shared oligopoly, and it is capex-linked, so it swings with fab construction.

The real differentiator, breadth plus spec-in: The 10-K's claim that "there are no global competitors that compete with us across the full range of our product offerings" is true and corroborated by the fragmented peer set — but ENTG competes niche-by-niche against a different specialist in each niche (Pall in filters, Fujifilm in slurry, DuPont/Qnity in pads, Shin-Etsu/Gudeng in carriers, Merck/Air Liquide/Linde in materials), and per-niche dominance is the exception (SDS, FOUPs), not the rule.❝ The real systems advantage is co-optimization: introducing a new material at deposition touches CMP, post-CMP cleans, etch, and filtration across stages, and only Entegris spans both the materials business and the purity/handling business under one roof — the molybdenum system is the proof point.❝ This monetizes as content and volume growth and stickiness, not as price.

3. Competitive advantages & moat assessment

Both segments earn excess returns today, earned and defensible node-by-node but open to erosion, with no returns premium over focused peers and a premium that is compressing rather than widening.❝ Neither is a structural, self-funding business of the Visa/Mastercard or Google-search type, and neither is a commodity price-taker. In business-model terms ENTG belongs with the qualified-consumable, switching-cost compounders — Thermo Fisher, Danaher (which owns Pall), Waters — that sell specified-in consumables into a validated customer process and grow through content and bolt-on M&A. The analogue holds on the switching cost and the recurring-consumable mechanism; it breaks on quality, because ENTG carries a larger cyclical, capex-linked hardware leg, a lower recurring-revenue share, and no returns premium over its focused peers.

3a. APS — durable in filtration, exposed in hardware

The case for an advantage: APS products are qualified as positions of record inside customers' recipes at the leading edge (2nm logic, ~300-layer NAND). Switching an in-recipe purifier risks yield on wafers worth far more than the consumable, so incumbency is sticky and priced through total cost of ownership. The financial evidence is visible: APS earned a segment-profit margin of 23.7-31.1% every year FY2019-FY2025 and a 44.7-47.0% gross margin, and in FY2025 it generated $426.4M of the company's $703.0M total segment profit — 61% of segment profit on 56% of sales.❝ Purity requirements ratchet up with each node shrink, so the consumable-filtration leg grew ~2% in a down year (FY2025) while the hardware leg declined 7%.

The case against a durable premium: The return premium is compressing, not widening. APS segment margin peaked at 31.1% in FY2022 (recast) and declined ~740 basis points to 23.7% by FY2025; gross margin slid from 47.0% to 44.7%. A strengthening moat widens the premium; APS narrowed it.❝ Part is cyclical (the fab-capex-linked hardware leg), part is $21.9M of FY2025 restructuring and plant under-utilization, and part is the ~$38M depreciation step-up from capacity built ahead of volume. APS also reinvests heavily to hold position — company R&D 10.3% of sales, capex 9.4% (13.0% in FY2023) — and it is the more capital-intensive of the two segments, which is not the profile of a top-band, self-funding business. And the hardware leg is a competitive oligopoly: two firms claim FOUP leadership, and Taiwan pure-play Gudeng grew revenue ~35% in 2024 into EUV/reticle pods and FOUPs while Entegris's FOUP line was declining — share moving toward an aggressive local specialist in the products where APS shrank.

Management claims: spec-in switching costs ("switching away from our products may be costly and time-consuming… may introduce risk to their manufacturing yields"), content per wafer rising with complexity, POR wins at 2nm, microenvironment leadership, and breadth (no full-range competitor) (FY2025 10-K, Item 1). External sources: Shin-Etsu Polymer markets a large 300mm FOUP/FOSB share; Gudeng grew group revenue from ~US$162M (2024 forecast) toward realized levels above that, expanding into EUV pods; Mersen's advanced-materials operating margin declined from 15.7% (2023) to 9.1% (2025) on the SiC-semiconductor downturn, confirming the hardware/materials layer is deeply cyclical ([web], accessed 2026-07-07). Ground-up view: filtration and purification have a real, recurring switching cost (the buyer's cost of failure dwarfs the consumable, and re-qualifying costs months plus yield risk), while microenvironment is a natural oligopoly of a few molders where lower-cost local challengers can and do take share, and concentrated buyer power (TSMC 16%, top-10 50%) caps pricing power across the board. The three groundings reconcile to: durable in filtration and purification, exposed and cyclical in handling.

3b. MS — a real switching-cost edge with no returns premium and a rising Chinese challenger

The case for an edge: On-wafer materials are qualified into a recipe, and re-qualifying an alternative takes 12-24+ months and risks yield, so a qualified position is sticky and the revenue is consumable.❝ The margin profile: a 43.8% segment gross margin, a 19.7% segment-profit margin (~26% at the segment EBITDA line [estimate]), and ~17% after-tax return on tangible operating capital [estimate]. Breadth is the second real strength — MS spans deposition, CMP, post-CMP clean, and etch, and can co-optimize a whole process module when a new material like molybdenum is introduced, which no single competitor matches.

Three facts keep MS below the top tier: First, no margin premium over focused peers, and the gap runs the wrong way. Qnity Electronics (the DuPont electronics spin, the cleanest comparable) earned a 29.5% adjusted operating-EBITDA margin on $4,754M of FY2025 sales growing +10% organic; Merck's EMD Electronics ran ~25.5% EBITDA-pre. MS's ~26% estimated segment-EBITDA margin is in-line-to-below that band while MS grew low-single-digit — the opposite pattern of a widening moat.❝ Second, each line faces an equal-or-larger specialist competitor, and in the flagship CMP-slurry line China's Anji Microelectronics lifted global slurry share from ~7% (2022) to ~11% (2024) with revenue +48%, directly against MS's largest line, while the switching-cost moat resets at every node transition.❝ Third, Entegris paid full price for the half of MS it bought (~$3,631M of goodwill from a ~5.4x sales / ~17x EBITDA deal), so on full invested capital MS earns an estimated ~2% ROIC — the excess return was capitalized into the purchase price.❝ The operating business earns well above its cost of capital on the assets it uses (~17% on tangible capital); the gap between ~17% and ~2% is what shareholders paid for the CMC half.

3c. Returns tested three ways

Standalone: Consolidated GAAP operating margin has been 14-24% over ten years (average ~17%), and reported ROIC collapsed from ~18-21% (2017-2021) to ~5% (2022-2025). The collapse is a capital-load effect, not operating deterioration: CMC increased invested capital from ~$2.2B (2021) to ~$8.4B, adding ~$4.85B of goodwill and intangibles. On tangible invested capital (~$2.4B, ex-goodwill/intangibles) the business still earns ~16%❝ — so the operating assets compound at mid-teens, while the ~$6.0B CMC price drags reported returns to ~5% until ENTG grows into it. FCF margin recovered to 12.4% in FY2025 as the capex build rolled off.

ROIC / cost-of-capital definitions. ROIC = normalized NOPAT ÷ year-end invested capital, where NOPAT = GAAP operating income × (1 − 15% normalized tax rate) — ENTG's GAAP effective rate ran 7-9% recently on international/FDII structure, so a 15% normalized rate is used and stated. Invested capital = total equity + total borrowings − cash. Cost-of-capital anchor: a high-single-digit WACC (~8-9%) given ~$3.7B of debt at a ~5-6% blended rate plus equity. This is a business-quality test, not a stock valuation.

4. Competitive landscape & market sizing

ENTG competes not in one market but in a set of adjacent oligopolies, each populated by a different specialist.❝ The 10-K states plainly that "there are no global competitors that compete with us across the full range of our product offerings" — a statement about breadth, not single-line dominance. Where ENTG leads and where it trails is the substance.

Binding constraint and bargaining power: The primary constraint is demand-side — revenue is gated by wafer volumes and by being qualified into a concentrating set of leading-edge fabs — confirmed by the FY2025 result (APS's capex-linked hardware declined with fab-construction capex). The financial metric to watch is gross margin, which held ~44-46% through the 2023-2025 downturn but turned up in FY2026 partly on the useful-life accounting change, not underlying pricing. There is no circular financing of concern: intersegment demand is small (~$9M), there are no equity stakes in customers, and customer prepayments are modest ($49.9M).

Customer-side economics: A leading-edge 300mm wafer sells for ~$18,000-20,000 [web] and yields hundreds of high-value dies; a single contamination-driven killer defect scraps dies worth far more than the ENTG consumable that would have prevented it. This asymmetry — a few dollars of filtration or precursor protecting thousands of dollars of wafer value — is why ENTG sustains a 44% gross margin against buyers as concentrated as TSMC, and it scales with node complexity, adding content per wafer at each transition.❝

6. Secular & industry trends

Entegris's end-markets are pulled by one durable force: rising materials intensity and purity requirements per wafer as chips move to smaller geometries and new architectures (2nm/GAA logic, 3D-NAND layer scaling, HBM/advanced packaging, new interconnect metals). Materials spending per wafer roughly doubles across a node transition (logic 28nm→7nm ~2.2x; 3D NAND 64→128 layers ~1.8x), and the metal-impurity limit tightens ~1000x from 28nm to 7nm❝, which is the physics behind "content per wafer" compounding (Investor Day 2018, slides 11, 23). That force is real and is why management has claimed ~4-5 points of organic outgrowth over the industry for a decade.

The problem for the current view: the outgrowth has not been visible in consolidated results for three years. Net sales went $3,523.9M (FY2023) → $3,241.2M (FY2024) → $3,196.6M (FY2025), roughly flat-to-down, and FY2025 unit-driven revenue grew only ~2%, which management itself described as "in line with wafer starts," i.e. zero consolidated outgrowth❝ (Q4 2025 call). Management attributes the stall to three things it frames as cyclical or portfolio, not secular: ~$1B of chosen divestitures removed revenue; a mainstream-logic and fab-construction downcycle hit ~1/3 of the business; and China (~21% of sales) is capped by export controls and local competition.

Share math across two eras: In the pre-CMC decade (2015→2021, $1,081M→$2,299M, ~13.4% reported CAGR including bolt-ons) against an industry growing high-single-digits in dollars and mid-single-digits in silicon area, a ~4-5 point organic outgrowth is broadly credible; management's disclosed pairs (2019 industry units ~−7% versus ENTG organic ~−3%) corroborate it. In the post-CMC era (2023→2025) the outgrowth disappeared at the consolidated level: net sales declined 9.3% cumulatively while wafer starts were roughly flat-to-up. The outgrowth is real but now concentrated at the leading edge — the departing CEO noted "very significant growth at TSMC… We have not seen that anywhere else" (Bernstein, May 2025), which is the vulnerability: the secular engine currently runs on one customer and region. The sharpest external cut came from an analyst, not a filing: Bernstein's Stacy Rasgon argued "all the growth in the industry over the last 5 years has been price… units in '24 were effectively flat… unit growth was 0," and the CEO agreed pricing "has been essentially flat" (Bernstein, May 2025). If industry wafer units have been roughly flat for five years, ENTG's ~75%-unit-driven revenue has had almost no volume tailwind, so the entire outgrowth has had to come from content-per-wafer share gain plus M&A — which netted to ~0 consolidated for three years.❝

Why the base is weak — the demand is trailing-edge: the ~75%-unit-driven engine tracks wafer starts (MSI), and MSI is ~90% mainstream/mature, so ENTG's volume is a trailing-edge bet; the 2023-2025 stall was the mature-node post-COVID inventory correction, which hit wafer VOLUME (SEMI silicon-area shipments fell ~16.6% peak-to-trough 2022→2024) even as equipment SPENDING (+10% to a 2024 record) and industry DOLLARS (WSTS ~+51%) rose on AI and price. The peer growth cohort confirms the exposure: ex-divestiture, ENTG grew like the wafer-volume names (Cabot -14%, Element ~0%, MKS ~flat), not the leading-edge names (KLA +32%, AMAT +10%), and slower than the purest leading-edge materials peers (Qnity +10% organic, Merck EMD +7.8%) — a content-per-wafer engine would have grown like KLA; it grew like Cabot. On memory (~22% of revenue, unit-driven), incumbent DRAM/NAND wafer capacity ex-China was held flat-to-down 2020-2025 (bit demand met by node migration and layer stacking, not more wafers) and most net-new DRAM wafers went to China (CXMT ~40k→~280k wpm), which ENTG is partly export-capped out of, so its memory-wafer base barely grew until the HBM build now beginning (mostly 2H2026-2028). Full data and sources in Appendix D.

The forward bet: leading-edge content-per-wafer gains reignite the outgrowth — the 2nm/GAA logic ramp (fab utilization at advanced logic "already near 100%"), NAND layer scaling from ~250 toward ~300 layers driving double-digit content per wafer (though incremental wafer starts lag to 2027), strong DRAM/HBM on AI, and molybdenum's cross-portfolio pull. Q1 2026 (+5% total, +7% unit-driven versus a mid-to-high-single-digit MSI call) is the first quarter that supports it, but it is one quarter. The company's own TAM projection has been upgraded from ~$1.0T (2022) to ~$1.6T by 2030 even as its own revenue stalled, so the industry-lift case strengthened while company capture did not, in the same window.❝

Cyclical versus secular: The business rides two cycles bolted onto the content trend. The unit-driven ~75% tracks wafer starts (the shallower cycle — organic only ~−3% in the 2019 down-year versus industry units ~−7%). The capex-driven ~25% (two-thirds fab construction, one-third WFE) tracks fab build-out (the deeper cycle — declined 7% in FY2025). As of the run date the cycle sits in early recovery, so recent growth is more cycle (recovery off a 2023-2024 trough) than proven trend re-acceleration; the secular content layer is the call option on top, visible in specific leading-edge lines (liquid filtration record quarters, double-digit advanced-deposition and selective-etch growth) but not yet in the consolidated top line. The KPIs that would flash first if the secular trend were breaking, ranked: unit-driven revenue falling below management's cited MSI for two-plus quarters; a liquid-filtration deceleration; advanced-deposition and selective-etch growth rolling over; POR losses at 2nm/next-gen NAND/HBM nodes; and China revenue underperforming the China market after local-for-local supply is assured. External data on memory pricing that would inform this is available on demand (Appendix C).

7. Critical points

  1. The forward case is a bet on the semiconductor cycle plus a few points of content-per-wafer outgrowth, and the single largest lever is a macro input Entegris does not control. In the driver model, a ±2-point move in wafer-start growth (MSI, which hits ~75% of revenue) swings FY2035 adjusted operating income by ~$380M — larger than any margin lever. About 25% of revenue is even more cyclical, tied to fab-construction capex, which declined 7% in FY2025. The business is high-quality and consumable-heavy, but its top line is gated by wafer volumes and by being qualified into a concentrated set of leading-edge fabs, so the multi-year outcome rests on the cycle and on content outgrowth reappearing after netting to ~0 consolidated for three years.

  2. The 2022 CMC Materials acquisition has excellent strategic logic and delivered integration, but its price and timing turned the returns record. Reported ROIC collapsed from ~20% (2021) to ~5% (2022-2025) purely on the ~$4.85B of goodwill and intangibles the ~$6.0B, ~17x-EBITDA, cycle-peak deal loaded onto the balance sheet; on tangible capital the business still earns ~16%. GAAP diluted EPS in FY2025 ($1.55) is about half its 2021 pre-CMC peak ($3.00) four years later, non-GAAP EPS ($2.75) sits below its 2022 peak, and the "~60% of FCF to shareholders" policy has been suspended since 2022 to service ~$3.7B of debt. Enterprise revenue grew 172% over the decade, mostly via M&A, but that growth did not reach the per-share earnings line.

  3. A material part of the FY2026 margin improvement is a non-operating accounting change. A January-2026 extension of PP&E useful lives cut FY2026 depreciation by ~$73M company-wide and lifted gross margin; by the CEO's own bridge, ~150 basis points of the ~275-basis-point gross-margin improvement from Q4 2025 to Q2 2026 is the useful-life change and ~125 basis points is operational (Q1 2026 call). It is real and permanent but EBITDA-neutral, so it flatters GAAP margins and segment margins — especially the capital-heavy APS — without adding to underlying operating leverage. Consensus's ~49% forward gross margin and ~26.5% adjusted operating margin should be weighed with this split in mind.

  4. APS is not a uniform annuity; it is a high-margin consumable-filtration jewel bolted to a lower-margin, cyclical hardware business. The pre-recast split shows filtration and purification at ~35% segment margin (consumable, unit-driven, ~60% of APS) and materials handling at ~18% (capex-linked, ~40%). The 17-point intra-segment gap means blended APS margin is a mix number that swings with cycle position, which is why it compressed ~740 basis points from its 2022 peak as fab-construction capex declined. The durable moat is on the filtration leg; the hardware leg is where Gudeng and China localization are taking share.

  5. The operating leverage the forward margin case assumes for Materials Solutions is not yet visible, the most-disputed driver. On the cleanest quarterly comparison, Q1 2025 to Q1 2026, MS revenue rose $9.7M but segment profit rose only $0.9M, and gross profit declined $1.1M even with an $8.2M depreciation tailwind. Consensus prices ENTG's adjusted operating margin into the semicap-oligopoly band (~26.5% by 2028), which no materials or consumables peer in the reference class has durably occupied and which sits above ENTG's own all-time adjusted peak. This is where the internal-versus-external gap bites (see Section 8 and Section 14).

  6. Customer and geographic concentration is high and rising, on both axes. TSMC is 16% of net sales (from 11% in 2023), the top-10 is 50% (from 43%), Taiwan is ~23% and China ~21%, and international sales reached 82%. The 10-K newly flags customers demanding "dual sourcing or rapid qualification of alternative products" and harsher commercial terms, and one analyst noted ENTG trailed TSMC's own 2025 growth. Concentration braked by qualification switching costs still leaves the revenue base exposed to a single customer's capex timing and to a two-sided squeeze in a downturn (rising buyer power plus scarce-input supplier power).

  7. Deleveraging is real but ran roughly two years behind schedule, and per-share value creation has stalled. Gross debt was cut from ~$5.9B (2022) to $3.7B (2025) and ~$2.1B repaid, but the committed sub-4x-gross-by-2024 target slipped, net leverage was still 3.6x at Q1 2026 (target ~3x by end-2026), and buybacks and M&A remain gated on hitting ~2-3x. In 2022 ENTG issued ~12.9M shares as CMC currency at ~$92-133, more shares than the ~10.2M repurchased over six years at a ~$39 blended price, so a single deal's equity issuance more than reversed the buyback history at a far higher price.

  8. In advanced packaging — a fast-growing AI-materials vector — Entegris is a follower, by its own admission. Run-rate is only ~$100M (~3% of sales), most new products are "sampling now" with benefit skewed to 2027+❝, and the CEO conceded "our exposure to advanced packaging right now is limited due to just the prior investments that we didn't make" (Q1 2026 call). An analyst framed it as "one of the variables between you and your closest peer." This undercuts the "we win where complexity rises" framing where complexity is rising fastest, and is a live variable against the near-term bull case.

  9. The team giving guidance today is new, and the standing long-range framework has over-promised at every cycle peak. CEO Dave Reeder (August 2025) and CFO Sukhi Nagesh (May 2026) are both new; the last two long-range models missed badly (the September-2022 model of ~$5.2B revenue and >$6 EPS by 2025 came in at $3.2B / $2.75, ~38% and ~55% short), and both FY2024 and FY2025 annual frameworks missed. A reset Capital Markets Day is set for November 2026; the base rate on this company's peak-set long-range targets is that they over-predict, which is the calibration to apply to whatever the new team sets.

Each point below is a place where the evidence cuts against the consensus or management framing, quantified.

  1. "High-quality secular compounder" versus the realized three-year record. Over the trailing three years ENTG returned +27.6% while the semicap complex it is grouped with returned +150% to +430% (AMAT +297%, Lam +428%, KLA +375%), and GAAP diluted EPS declined from $3.00 (2021) to $1.55 (2025).   3. Materials Solutions shows no margin premium over its cleanest peer, and the gap runs the wrong way. The softer part of the framing is the "widening" direction: the gap to Qnity is adverse today, but calling it a widening structural trend rests on one fresh-spin year of Qnity data, so the substance is "no premium and an adverse level," stated as such rather than as a proven widening.

  4. Materials Solutions is not clearly the scale leader in its two largest lines. Management's breadth claim is accurate, but MS is #2 in combined CMP consumables (behind pad-dominant DuPont/Qnity) and challenged in slurry by Fujifilm, and sub-scale to Merck-plus-Air-Liquide (~35% combined) in deposition precursors; it leads only in sub-atmospheric implant-gas delivery. The precise product-line rankings come from third-party market research and are directional, not filing-grade [web, low-med]; the firmer point is the 10-K's own statement that "there are no global competitors that compete with us across the full range," which is a breadth claim, not a single-line dominance claim.

  5. In advanced packaging Entegris is a follower, not a leader. The Q1 2026 transcript supports the substantive claims: an analyst framed advanced packaging as "one of the variables between you and your closest peer," the CEO said exposure "is limited due to just the prior investments that we didn't make," run-rate is ~$100M, and benefit is skewed to 2027+. The framing that ENTG is broadly behind should stay scoped to advanced packaging specifically; in its core lines (filtration, FOUPs, slurries, implant gas) it is a leader or co-leader.

  6. Consistent EPS beats coincide with guidance-driven selling. ENTG beat non-GAAP EPS in 10 of the last 12 quarters, but the stock declined on 8 of 12 prints, and four EPS beats produced same-day declines greater than 5% (Q1'24 −7.2%, Q2'25 −14.6%, Q3'25 −7.4%, Q1'26 −5.3%). The mean one-day reaction was ≈ −2.7% on a mean EPS surprise of ≈ +5.7%. The market trades the forward guide and margins, not the beat, and some beats are lower quality (the Q1 2026 beat leaned on an ~8% non-GAAP tax rate including an unforecasted reserve release, versus a ~15% guide).

  7. "China largely de-risked" is scoped to supply, not demand. Management calls China "largely de-risked," but that refers to supply assurance (~85% of China revenue now supplied from Asia facilities, targeting >90%), not to demand or competition. China is ~$670M (21%) of sales, export-control- and competition-exposed, with dollars already declining slightly, and the de-risking is itself a costly, margin-dilutive local-for-local build. Anji took global CMP-slurry share from ~7% to ~11% in two years.

  8. The base forward case leans optimistic in three places. MS unit-driven growth (base +5.7%/yr) sits above the demonstrated ~2-3% run-rate and leans on a 2027 memory step-up the June-2026 SK Hynix HBM slowdown puts in question, so the evidence favors the low half of the range. The MS segment margin base (23%) is above any post-CMC full-year peak (20.4%) and above the level the not-yet-visible operating leverage supports (~21-22%). And incremental working capital (base 15% of the revenue change) sits at the very bottom of ENTG's realized 15-42% range, so it likely understates cash use in growth years and overstates FCF. These are calibration cautions on the base, not refutations; they push the central case toward the lower half of its growth and margin ranges.

10. Management, incentives & capital allocation

Leadership in the middle of its deepest refresh in a decade: Bertrand Loy ran Entegris from November 2012 to August 2025 (~13 years) and led both major acquisitions; he stays as Executive Chair through July 31, 2026. David Reeder, 51, became CEO in August 2025 after ~18 months as a director — a career CFO (GlobalFoundries, then a short stint at Chewy) with only a brief prior large-cap CEO seat (Lexmark). The CFO seat has turned over twice in ~3 years: Graves (retired 2023) to LaGorga (separation January 2026) to Sukhi Nagesh (effective May 2026, an external hire Reeder previously worked with at GlobalFoundries, with M&A/corporate-development experience, which one analyst took as a signal ENTG may resume acquisitions once net leverage clears ~3x). New leadership is finance- and operations-oriented, consistent with the 2025-2026 pivot to cash flow, footprint rationalization, and deleveraging.

Compensation: About 92% of CEO target pay is variable. Long-term incentive mix was 40% time-vested RSUs / 30% time-vested options / 30% performance stock units, "unchanged since 2015," with the PSU tied to a single metric — three-year relative total shareholder return versus the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (capped at target if absolute TSR is negative). The annual bonus for FY2025 weighted Adjusted EBITDA Margin 50%, Revenue Growth in excess of markets 25% (which paid 0.0% — the company failed to outgrow its market), Free Cash Flow Margin 15%, and Customer Experience 10%, paying 69% of target. "Adjusted EBITDA" excludes intangible amortization, integration, restructuring, and divestiture P&L — it strips out the costs of the debt-funded deals that built the EBITDA base and sits above the ~$200M interest burden. For the full CMC era (2022-2025) no long-term-incentive metric touched leverage, FCF, ROIC, or per-share value — the exact outcomes management publicly promised and repeatedly missed. A 2026 overhaul, arriving only after say-on-pay support dropped to 77.2% (from 91.2%), raises PSU weight to 60% (CEO), adds a three-year FCF-margin PSU metric, and eliminates options. Reeder's package is a peer-median ~$13.3M target with make-whole-only sign-on equity and no inducement awards; Loy's Executive-Chair bridge is a rich ~$6.6M for a ~1-year advisory role.

Insider ownership: All directors and officers together hold ~0.53% of shares; Loy's ~393,000 shares (~$33M) are the meaningful stake, while Reeder personally holds 1,833 shares (~$154K) plus ~$11M of unvested make-whole equity. Over the five years to end-2025 ENTG delivered a negative cumulative total shareholder return ($100 to $89) while its compensation peer group nearly tripled ($100 to $268) — the gap that drove the say-on-pay drop.

Candor: The clearest test is the CMC deleveraging and EPS path, which was walked down repeatedly: from ">$6 EPS by 2025" and "3.5x gross by 2023" (September 2022) to ">$5 by 2026" and "sub-4x by 2024" (February 2024), with actuals of net leverage 3.8x at end-2025 and non-GAAP EPS $2.75. On the other side, management explicitly flagged that part of the FY2026 margin lift is the useful-life accounting change rather than operations, and put free cash flow into the incentive plan — aligning the metric the new team emphasizes with pay.

11. M&A, divestitures & deal track record

Entegris is a disciplined, technology-motivated serial acquirer, not a value-destroyer — with the caveat that its one transformational deal was mispriced and mistimed.❝ Over 2014-2022 it bought nine specialty-materials and filtration platforms plus CMC Materials, none of the tuck-ins has been impaired, and the discipline is visible in the deal it did not do (Versum). Structural risks, ranked: (1) The 2029 refinancing concentration plus coupon step-up (~$40-55M/yr latent), partly mitigated by the IG-rated secured tranche and the upgrade path. (2) Negative tangible book equity — goodwill $3,947M plus intangibles $907M ($4,854M) exceed total equity $3,953M, so tangible book is roughly −$900M (−$760M at Q1 2026); a goodwill impairment (as in 2023) hits equity but not cash or covenants. (3) Small unhedged floating exposure ($400M, ~$4M per 100bp), to be retired in 2026. (4) Offshore cash concentration (U.S. cash only $121M). (5) Covenant risk is low — the revolver is undrawn (springing covenant untested), the notes have only incurrence covenants, and the term loan is covenant-lite.

18. Risks

Severity-ranked; each with mechanism, quantified exposure, and the leading indicator that would flash first. The strongest signal is change: the FY2025 10-K elevated AI to a standalone risk factor, expanded the tariff, customer-concentration, and China export-control factors, and retired COVID.

Tier 1 — structural, large exposure

  1. Semiconductor cyclicality with disproportionate operating leverage. ~75% of revenue tracks wafer starts and ~25% tracks fab-construction/WFE capex; "a lower volume of sales can have a large and disproportionate impact on our profitability because some of our expenses are fixed" (FY2025 10-K). The whole ~$3.2B base is gated by wafer volumes; capex-driven organic declined 7% in FY2025. Leading indicator: MSI/wafer-start growth, fab utilization (advanced logic near 100%, mainstream 75-80%), fab-construction capex.

  2. Customer concentration — TSMC 16%, top-10 = 50% and rising. No or limited contractual recourse if a customer stops buying; being designed out of a node cuts revenue sharply and is slow to replace. TSMC ~$512M, top-10 ~$1.6B; concentration rose 43%→48%→50% over 2023-2025, and the 10-K newly flags customers pushing dual-sourcing and harsher terms. Leading indicator: qualification of a second source; ENTG growth versus its lead customer's (an analyst flagged ENTG trailed TSMC's 2025 growth).

  3. China — export controls, local-competitor substitution, input retaliation (~21% of sales). Export controls cut addressable demand and incentivize local sourcing; China's 2025 critical-mineral restrictions threaten ENTG's own inputs. China ~$670M, dollars already declining; "de-risked" refers to supply, not demand. Leading indicator: China revenue trend; local-for-local qualification %; Anji-type share gains in CMP/filtration.

  4. High leverage from CMC — $3.7B gross debt, ~$190M annual interest. Debt service consumes cash and limits flexibility; refinancing the 2028-2030 maturities at higher rates is a +$40-55M/yr latent headwind. Net leverage 3.6x; target ~3x by end-2026. Leading indicator: net leverage, FCF, ratings, refinancing coupons.

Tier 2 — material

  1. Goodwill impairment — $3,947M goodwill (123% of revenue); MS cushion narrowing. A downturn or competitive loss can trigger impairment; the FY2025 10-K moved the MS reporting unit to quantitative testing with a sensitivity disclosure, signaling a thinner fair-value cushion at MS than APS. Precedent: $115.2M impaired in 2023. Leading indicator: MS margin trend, the annual August test, held-for-sale decisions.

  2. Margin quality — the reported gross-margin expansion is partly accounting-driven. By the CEO's own bridge, ~150 of the ~275 basis points of gross-margin improvement from Q4 2025 to Q2 2026 is the January-2026 useful-life change, not operations, and new-facility ramps (KSP Taiwan, Colorado) are dilutive through 2027. Leading indicator: normalized (ex-useful-life) gross margin; facility break-even timing; depreciation run-rate.

  3. Advanced-packaging under-exposure. In the fastest-growing AI-materials sub-market, ENTG is a follower at ~$100M run-rate with admitted prior underinvestment and benefit skewed to 2027+, while a closest peer leads. Leading indicator: advanced-packaging revenue growth; POR wins in HBM/hybrid-bonding CMP, TSV, carriers.

  4. NAND recovery lag and mainstream-logic softness. ENTG's NAND benefit is layer-count content, not wafer-start growth, which stays "measured"; mainstream logic (~30% of revenue) recovery is slow and still below the 2022 peak, and management flags memory shortages could destroy mainstream/consumer demand in 2H 2026. Leading indicator: NAND wafer starts versus layer mix; mainstream MSI; memory pricing pass-through.

Tier 3 — present, lower or idiosyncratic

  1. Supply chain — sole/single/limited-source inputs plus PFAS. Loss of a sole source (membranes, resins, abrasive particles, petroleum coke, specialty gases) or a PFAS restriction could halt production; re-qualification is slow. PFAS exposure is unsized — ENTG's core APS products (FOUPs, membranes, tubing, valves) are fluoropolymer-based. Mitigated by multi-year supply agreements. Leading indicator: raw-material inflation, PFAS rulemaking.

  2. Tariffs / trade-policy whipsaw. Tariffs raise cost and distort order patterns (2025 pull-forwards depressed 2026 China comps); February 2026 the Supreme Court invalidated IEEPA tariffs but new Section-301 investigations opened. Leading indicator: tariff rulings, order-pattern volatility.

  3. Leadership transition / strategy in flux. New CEO, new CFO, new IR head, and a Capital Markets Day postponed to November 2026; continuity/execution risk on the margin-expansion and deleveraging plans, and the M&A-oriented CFO hire signals possible return to M&A in 2027. Leading indicator: strategy consistency, M&A activity.

  4. Hazardous materials / product liability. ENTG manufactures specialty chemicals and ships toxic implant gases; an explosion, spill, or gas leak could cause injury and liability, and "our insurance coverage may be inadequate" — a low-probability, high-severity tail.

Correlations — the bad quarter: A semiconductor downturn lights up several risks at once: it cuts the concentrated customer base and utilization (R1+R2), can trigger a goodwill impairment where the MS cushion is thin (R5), reduces FCF against fixed ~$190M interest and stalls deleveraging (R4), and reverses fixed-cost absorption, exposing the accounting-flattered margin once the useful-life tailwind annualizes (R6). Separately, a China shock correlates the demand hit (R3) with tariff whipsaw (R10) and critical-mineral input retaliation (R9) — a demand and a cost/supply hit at the same time, concentrated in ~21% of revenue.

  2. Is the CMC revenue-synergy thesis working?   4. Does Entegris hold the ruthenium interconnect position, or only molybdenum? Molybdenum is the single biggest unshipped-technology bet in the bull case, but ruthenium is the competing next-metal candidate for some advanced-logic layers; if ruthenium wins layers where ENTG is not specified in, part of the content-per-wafer upside accrues to a competitor. APS ROIC was left "not computable" while MS was triangulated (~2% full / ~17% tangible), so the "APS is the jewel" framing is a margin claim, not a returns claim — and APS is the more capital-intensive segment, so its margin premium could compress on a returns basis. No standalone margin, growth, or competitive analysis (versus Mersen, SGL Carbon), so the "narrowing to pure semi" divestiture strategy and its margin/return-mix effect cannot be evaluated.
