D1 (2026-07-11): Does ENTG track trailing-edge wafer volume more than leading-edge content-per-wafer, and did memory ex-China stagnate?

Question. A friend challenges the dossier's growth framing: (1) ENTG's wafer volumes are driven primarily by lagging-edge/trailing-edge/mature-node wafers rather than leading-edge, so its revenue growth has lagged the broader semiconductor industry — because mature nodes suffered a deep post-COVID inventory correction (roughly 2023-2025) that leading-edge/AI demand did not; and (2) memory wafers ex-China have not grown meaningfully until now. Are both claims true, and if so is the dossier's content-per-wafer / leading-edge growth framing (§1, §6, §13.4, §17) incomplete? What should change?

The friend is directionally right, and the leading-edge/content-per-wafer framing is a material nuance the dossier under-weights. The precise version splits into two denominators the dossier collapses into one.

By revenue ENTG is not majority-mature; by wafer volume it is. ENTG discloses no audited node split anywhere in its filings; the only node-level figures are management's approximate, verbal end-market cuts, which sum to slightly over 100% across the two most recent calls — advanced logic ~40% / mainstream logic ~30% / memory ~30% in Q4-2025, and ~40% / ~30% / "about a third" in Q1-2026 (ENTG Q4-2025 and Q1-2026 earnings calls). Weighted by revenue, "more than 60% of Entegris' revenue comes from advanced logic and advanced memory," advanced logic being management's own ≤7nm definition (Q4-2025 call), so the best-supported bound on the mature/mainstream-plus-non-semi share of revenue is ~35-45%, roughly 40%. Content-per-wafer re-weights the book toward the leading edge because materials spend per wafer is roughly 2x higher there (logic 28nm→7nm ~2.2x; 3D-NAND 64L→128L ~1.8x, ENTG 2018/2020 investor decks). So the claim is too strong if it implies ENTG's revenue is majority-mature. What the dossier omits is the other denominator: by wafer VOLUME the majority of the wafers ENTG's products run on are mature/mainstream. Management itself says "mainstream is the bulk of the logic market in terms of MSI" and that AI is "5% of the wafer starts market" though ENTG content on those wafers is much higher (Q4-2025 call). About 75% of revenue is unit-driven and "correlated to MSI" — total, mature-dominated wafer starts (Q4-2025 call). The proof is realized, not theoretical: FY2025 unit-driven revenue grew "approximately 2%... in line with wafer starts," zero content-per-wafer outgrowth at the consolidated level despite a booming leading edge (Q4-2025 call). The audited customer-category split shows the same thing — the Fabs line (62% of 2025 sales, the unit-driven consumables core) grew only +2.4% then +0.4% in 2024/2025 (FY2025 10-K Note 2), and ENTG's revenue from its largest foundry customer was "flat to modestly up" in 2025, trailing that customer's own AI-driven growth (Q1-2026 call).

The industry structure confirms the mechanism. Two cycles decoupled hard. Wafer VOLUME (SEMI silicon-area shipments) declined from a 2022 record of 14,713 MSI to 12,266 MSI in 2024 — about -16.6% peak-to-trough, -14.3% in 2023 alone — before a +5.8% recovery to 12,973 MSI in 2025 (SEMI silicon-shipment releases [web]). Equipment SPENDING barely moved: total billings went $107.6B (2022) → $106.3B (2023, -1.3%) → a record $117.1B (2024, +10%) (SEMI equipment-billings releases [web]). The volume correction was a mature-node event — utilization declined from ~100% to the ~70% range on weak auto/industrial/consumer demand, while leading-edge held ~100% on AI (TSMC 3nm/5nm sold out; about 69% of TSMC's Q3-2024 wafer revenue came from ≤7nm nodes) (TrendForce, marklapedus, TSMC Q3-2024 filing [web]). Structurally, sub-7nm was ~850,000 wpm in 2024, roughly 10% of total capacity — so ~90% of wafer volume is mainstream/mature, which is exactly why the aggregate wafer-start series a consumables franchise tracks moved with the mature cycle, not the AI cycle (SEMI 300mm fab outlook [web]).

Did ENTG underperform? Against industry dollars yes, against its own driver no. Reported net sales declined 9.3% over 2023→2025 ($3,524M → $3,197M) while WSTS industry dollars rose ~51% and wafer volume was still below its 2022 peak (financial_history.csv; WSTS [web]). But the entire reported decline is self-chosen divestitures — roughly $500M gone (Electronic Chemicals ~$360M/yr, PIM ~$150M/yr, QED, small-chem); ex-divestiture organic was about +5.7% cumulative (~+2.8%/yr), within a point of wafer volume (C&EN [web]; ma-analyst ledger). ENTG matched wafer starts with ~0 outgrowth. The peer benchmark confirms the friend's cut: ENTG grew like the wafer-volume cohort (Cabot -14%, Element +0%, MKS organic ~flat), not the leading-edge cohort (KLA +32%, AMAT +10%, LRCX +7%), and slower than the two purest leading-edge electronic-materials peers (Qnity +10% organic, Merck EMD Semiconductor Solutions +7.8% organic) (baserate_refclass.csv; peer disclosures [web]). A business whose engine were leading-edge content-per-wafer should have grown like KLA; it grew like Cabot.

Memory ex-China did stagnate. Incumbents (Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron; Kioxia in NAND) held wafer capacity flat-to-down 2020-2025, meeting bit demand by node migration (DRAM +35-70% bits/wafer per step) and NAND layer stacking (32→~420 layers), not more wafers, and in 2023 cut wafer starts hard (Micron ~-30% in both DRAM and NAND) (TrendForce; Micron FY2023 10-K [web]). SEMI's forecast has DRAM capacity +9%/yr and 3D NAND flat-to-+5% for 2024-2025, and most of even that went to China (CXMT DRAM ~40k→~280k wpm 2020→2025, supplying the majority of net new global DRAM wafers), which ENTG is partly export-capped out of (SEMI; Digitimes [web]). About 22-23% of ENTG revenue is memory AND unit-driven (~30% memory × ~75% unit-driven) and tracks that flat ex-China wafer base. The forward offset is HBM, whose 3-4x wafer intensity per usable bit finally forces net DRAM wafer additions, but the build-out (SK Hynix, Samsung, Micron greenfields) produces mostly 2H2026-2028 (TrendForce, io-fund [web]).

The dossier's framing is incomplete, and it points a reader at the wrong driver. The content-per-wafer narrative in §1 (unit economics), §6 (secular trends), §13.4 (KPIs) and §17 (forward model) is real at the product-line level but does not account for the consolidated 2023-2026 path: ENTG's demand tracks trailing-edge-weighted wafer VOLUME and ex-China memory far more than the leading-edge framing implies, because the ~75% unit-driven engine is bolted to total MSI (~90% mainstream/mature) and content-per-wafer delivered ~0 consolidated outgrowth for three years. What should change: §6 should decompose demand into wafer-VOLUME (the trailing-edge-weighted base ENTG tracks) versus industry-DOLLARS (the AI/memory value boom ENTG structurally under-participates in), and state that ENTG matched wafer starts with ~0 outgrowth 2023-2025. §1 and §13.4 should flag that the Fabs content-proxy category grew only +2.4%/+0.4% in 2024/2025. §17 should re-anchor the FY2026-2028 consensus (~9.4%/yr) on two things it currently treats as given — the wafer-volume recovery continuing (MSI +5.8% in 2025, mid-to-high-single-digit guided for 2026) and the RETURN of a content-per-wafer outgrowth absent for three years. Q1-2026 unit-driven +7%, running above the wafer-start rate for the first time, is the single data point that the outgrowth is coming back, aided by the HBM/ex-China wafer build now beginning (Q1-2026 call; quarterly_history) — but one quarter cannot prove durability, and off-trough re-acceleration in this class is reliably cyclical. Framed correctly, content-per-wafer is a forward call option on a wafer base that only just stopped being flat, not a demonstrated recent driver. Whether any of this bears on the stock's valuation is out of scope.
