All harvested proof points · company first, then peers

Evidence

Claims by section
business-model
  • About 75% of revenue is unit-driven — consumables consumed as wafers are made — so it tracks wafer volumes more than the more cyclical capital-equipment cycle, and about 25% is capex-driven (FOUPs and fluid-handling hardware tied to fab construction and wafer-fab-equipment spend)
  • The three-year segment path shows a crossing: MS margin expanded from 15.9% (FY2022) to 19.7% (FY2025) as low-margin divested lines left, while APS margin compressed from 31.1% (FY2022, recast) to 23.7% (FY2025) on soft capex-linked demand, restructuring, and a step-up in depreciation.
  • Switching away from a qualified filter, slurry, or precursor risks yield on wafers worth far more than the consumable, so the buyer optimizes for defectivity and supply assurance rather than unit price.
  • Contracts are short: supply agreements "typically do not include long-term purchase commitments," most do not exceed twelve months, and consolidated contract liabilities were only $49.9M at year-end, so there is no backlog annuity
  • Management sizes the long-run algorithm as industry units at roughly twice GDP plus 3-6 points of content-driven outgrowth
  • APS blends a high-margin (~35%), consumable, unit-driven filtration business with a lower-margin (~18%), capex-linked handling business.
  • APS sales declined 3% "mainly due to a decline in facilities-based capital expenditure investments," which cut demand for fluid-handling products and FOUPs, "partially offset by an increase in sales from gas and liquid filtration products
  • APS is the more capital-intensive of the two segments: its depreciation grew 49% over FY2023-FY2025, from $77.3M (4.2% of sales) to $115.5M (6.4%), because the Kaohsiung (Taiwan) and Colorado Springs plants came on line before the volume to fill them, which is the single largest identifiable driver of the margin compression.
  • MS is a consumables-and-materials business: a fab qualifies an MS slurry, precursor, gas, or etch chemistry into a specific process step, and thereafter every wafer running that step consumes MS product.
  • Fully loaded, MS is a ~8.6% GAAP operating-margin business [estimate], less than half its headline, and that is before its share of interest on the ~$3.7B of CMC debt.
  • On the cleanest apples-to-apples comparison, Q1 2025 to Q1 2026, MS revenue rose $9.7M but segment profit rose only $0.9M, and gross profit actually declined $1.1M even though the January-2026 useful-life change lowered MS depreciation by $8.2M that quarter — so ex-depreciation, underlying incremental gross margin was near zero to negative
capital-allocation
  • Over the decade capital allocation was M&A-led and debt-financed: cash M&A absorbed ~123% of operating cash flow (CMC alone 84% of it), capex 52%, and total shareholder returns just 19% — switched off entirely after 2022 to service debt.
competitive-advantage
  • Both segments earn excess returns today, earned and defensible node-by-node but open to erosion, with no returns premium over focused peers, and the margin premium each segment holds over its own history is compressing rather than widening.
  • APS earned a segment-profit margin of 23.7-31.1% every year FY2019-FY2025 and a 44.7-47.0% gross margin, and in FY2025 it generated $426.4M of the company's $703.0M total segment profit — 61% of segment profit on 56% of sales.
  • APS segment margin peaked at 31.1% in FY2022 (recast) and declined ~740 basis points to 23.7% by FY2025; gross margin slid from 47.0% to 44.7%. A strengthening moat widens the premium; APS narrowed it.
  • On-wafer materials are qualified into a recipe, and re-qualifying an alternative takes 12-24+ months and risks yield, so a qualified position is sticky and the revenue is consumable.
  • Qnity Electronics (the DuPont electronics spin, the cleanest comparable) earned a 29.5% adjusted operating-EBITDA margin on $4,754M of FY2025 sales growing +10% organic; Merck's EMD Electronics ran ~25.5% EBITDA-pre. MS's ~26% estimated segment-EBITDA margin is in-line-to-below that band while MS grew low-single-digit — the opposite pattern of a widening moat.
  • China's Anji Microelectronics lifted global slurry share from ~7% (2022) to ~11% (2024) with revenue +48%, directly against MS's largest line, while the switching-cost moat resets at every node transition.
  • Entegris paid full price for the half of MS it bought (~$3,631M of goodwill from a ~5.4x sales / ~17x EBITDA deal), so on full invested capital MS earns an estimated ~2% ROIC — the excess return was capitalized into the purchase price.
  • reported ROIC collapsed from ~18-21% (2017-2021) to ~5% (2022-2025). The collapse is a capital-load effect, not operating deterioration: CMC increased invested capital from ~$2.2B (2021) to ~$8.4B, adding ~$4.85B of goodwill and intangibles. On tangible invested capital (~$2.4B, ex-goodwill/intangibles) the business still earns ~16%
  • On a comparable consolidated basis, ENTG's 14.3% GAAP / ~20% ex-amortization operating margin is in line with or modestly above Danaher (19.1%) and clearly above cyclically-depressed Mersen and Shin-Etsu Polymer, but below the cleanest materials peer Qnity (29.5% EBITDA). ENTG is a solid, not exceptional, margin performer against this set.
  • Both segment premiums are eroding, against their own history and against competitors. APS's own margin premium compressed ~740 basis points from its FY2022 peak; the Q1 2026 recovery to 28.8% is strong but likely cyclical, and flattered ~160 basis points by the depreciation change, so the underlying is nearer ~27%.
competitive-landscape
  • ENTG competes not in one market but in a set of adjacent oligopolies, each populated by a different specialist.
  • Within APS, Entegris leads the two largest and richest sub-markets — filtration and purification (#1, ~28% of the liquid-filter market) and 300mm FOUPs/microenvironments (#1, ~28% of a ~$730M market) — and trails only in the small, fast-growing EUV/reticle-pod niche where Gudeng leads.
  • MS is five on-wafer lines competing against different peer sets. In its two largest lines it is #2, not #1. In CMP consumables (its flagship, ~half of MS), DuPont/Qnity is #1 (pad-dominant, >50% pad share) and Fujifilm leads copper slurry; MS is #2 in the combined market. In deposition precursors, Merck plus Air Liquide jointly hold ~35%; MS is a sub-scale, fast-growing entrant. MS leads structurally in exactly one line — sub-atmospheric implant-gas delivery (proprietary SDS/VAC).
  • TSMC is 16% of net sales and the top-10 is 50% and rising; concentrated, sophisticated buyers dual-source critical materials and push local-for-local supply, capping pricing power even where switching costs exist.
critical-points
  • Run-rate is only ~$100M (~3% of sales), most new products are "sampling now" with benefit skewed to 2027+
expectations
  • management gives no hard long-term model until a November-2026 Capital Markets Day, consensus embeds a cyclical recovery plus roughly a point a year of margin expansion, and the outside view says growth and margins in this reference class do not persist off a cycle peak outside the WFE oligopoly.
  • Management's word is highly reliable one quarter out — met-or-beat ~20 of 23 quarters on revenue and ~21 of 22 on EPS, deliberately conservative — but its annual and long-range models over-predicted three years running.
financial-history
  • Q1 2026 net sales of $812M were +5.0% YoY — the first clearly positive YoY quarter after a flat-to-down 2023-2025 — with gross margin at 46.9% and operating margin at 17.4%, both inflected up sharply versus the FY2025 full-year (44.4% and 14.3%).
  • Roughly 1-2 points of the gross-margin jump is the January-2026 useful-life change, which landed disproportionately in APS (segment margin 23.7% FY2025 to 28.8% in Q1 2026); MS did not get that accounting relief, and its Q1 2026 margin of 21.6% is a cleaner measure of the operating trend.
  • Fabs are now 62% of sales and the only growing category; the Q1 2026 divergence (Equipment & Engineering +20.6% on WFE recovery, Semi Distributor/Other −28.4% on China export-control drag) shows the WFE cycle turning while China stays weak.
m-and-a
  • Entegris is a disciplined, technology-motivated serial acquirer, though its one transformational deal was mispriced and mistimed.
  • The real test is CMC: strategically coherent (it completed the deposition-to-etch-to-polish-to-clean materials suite) and integration-executed (a $75M cost-synergy target hit by end-2023, ~$125M of savings later claimed), but struck at a 35% premium and ~5.4x sales / ~17-18x EBITDA at the December-2021 cyclical peak, funded with ~$3.7B of debt, with the deleveraging math running roughly two years behind.
secular-trends
  • Materials spending per wafer roughly doubles across a node transition (logic 28nm→7nm ~2.2x; 3D NAND 64→128 layers ~1.8x), and the metal-impurity limit tightens ~1000x from 28nm to 7nm
  • Net sales went $3,523.9M (FY2023) → $3,241.2M (FY2024) → $3,196.6M (FY2025), roughly flat-to-down, and FY2025 unit-driven revenue grew only ~2%, which management itself described as "in line with wafer starts," i.e. zero consolidated outgrowth
  • The company's own TAM projection has been upgraded from ~$1.0T (2022) to ~$1.6T by 2030 even as its own revenue stalled, so the industry-lift case strengthened while company capture did not, in the same window.
  • If industry wafer units have been roughly flat for five years, ENTG's ~75%-unit-driven revenue has had almost no volume tailwind, so the entire outgrowth has had to come from content-per-wafer share gain plus M&A — which netted to ~0 consolidated for three years.
technology
  • Entegris is not a single-technology moat like CUDA. It is a portfolio of tens of thousands of small, specified-in consumable products, where a "large product platform" is only ~$50M of revenue and no platform exceeds 3% of sales.
  • The moat is a proprietary adsorbent, a safety-qualification lock-in (a fab will not swap the system storing arsine in its cleanroom for a marginal saving), and an installed base of implant tools engineered around SDS.
  • Entegris built a cross-segment system — a ProE-Vap sublimation canister that turns the solid into gas, corrosion-resistant coatings for the gas lines, gas purification, and delivery cabinets — which a point competitor cannot easily match
  • This is the single largest unshipped-technology dependency in the forward case; if moly positions-of-record disappoint or push out a node, the content-per-wafer algorithm slows.
  • But it is a #2 position: third-party reports put Pall (Danaher) at ~35% of the semiconductor liquid-filter market versus Entegris ~28%
  • the fastest-growing adjacency — EUV/reticle pods — is led by ASML-certified, Taiwan-listed Gudeng, not Entegris.
  • ENTG competes niche-by-niche against a different specialist in each niche (Pall in filters, Fujifilm in slurry, DuPont/Qnity in pads, Shin-Etsu/Gudeng in carriers, Merck/Air Liquide/Linde in materials), and per-niche dominance is the exception (SDS, FOUPs), not the rule.
  • The real systems advantage is co-optimization: introducing a new material at deposition touches CMP, post-CMP cleans, etch, and filtration across stages, and only Entegris spans both the materials business and the purity/handling business under one roof. The molybdenum system is the working example.
value-chain
  • Entegris sits at a defensible middle node of the semiconductor chain: it buys largely commodity inputs (membranes, resins, abrasive particles, petroleum coke, base chemicals, specialty gases — several sole/single/limited-source) and sells them back as qualified, specified-in consumables at a 44.4% gross margin.
  • The two large below-the-line drains — $184.4M of amortization and $199.8M of interest — are both artifacts of the CMC deal, and they convert a healthy 44.4% gross margin into a 7.4% GAAP net margin, roughly one-sixth of TSMC's per-dollar net capture.
  • The chain's deepest, most durable profit pools sit at the ends — the leading-edge foundry and the WFE equipment oligopoly — not the middle.
  • This asymmetry — a few dollars of filtration or precursor protecting thousands of dollars of wafer value — is why ENTG sustains a 44% gross margin against buyers as concentrated as TSMC, and it scales with node complexity, adding content per wafer at each transition.
variant-perceptions
  • In a 13-name reference class, only the WFE oligopolists (AMAT ~28%, Lam ~29%) durably exceed ~24%; every materials/consumables peer sits lower (Cabot Microelectronics ~16%, Element ~11%, MKS ~11% through-cycle, ENTG's own ~17% GAAP average).
