Evidence

Claims by section
business-model
  • All wafers come from Flash Ventures, three jointly owned (49.9%) manufacturing ventures with Kioxia operating inside Kioxia's Yokkaichi and Kitakami fab complexes in Japan; SNDK buys wafers at cost plus a small markup, takes ~50% of output, and is contractually barred from fabbing flash anywhere else (10-K FY2025, Item 1).
  • SNDK is entitled to ~50% of Flash Ventures wafer output; bit growth comes from BiCS node transitions (more bits per wafer), not new fabs - "we can continue to drive the bit growth we're talking about mid- to high teens through nodal transitions" (Q3 FY2026 call, CEO).
  • Q3 FY2026 revenue rose 251% year over year on flat exabytes - ASP per gigabyte rose 248% (Q3 FY2026 10-Q MD&A).
  • its bits declined 40% year over year while its ASP rose 139%, and its revenue share declined from 33.7% of Q3 FY2025 to 13.8%. With total bits capped, Sandisk shipped to datacenter the bits it could have sold to consumers; consumer demand did not collapse.
  • sales-incentive and price-protection programs are netted from gross revenue and compressed from 19% of gross revenue in Q3 FY2025 to 12% in Q3 FY2026 (10-Q MD&A; FY2025/24/23 ran 19%/19%/21% per the 10-K), so realized price rose faster than list price.
  • enterprise SSD reached 25% of the portfolio in Q3 FY2026 from a mid-single-digit share a year earlier (Q3 FY2026 call; the year-ago figure is a Mizuho analyst estimate the CEO endorsed), with TLC roughly two-thirds and QLC one-third of the portfolio.
  • from Q3 FY2026, five multiyear supply agreements ("new business models", NBMs) with minimum purchase obligations began to firm the book - remaining performance obligations of $41.6B on the three contracts signed in the quarter, financial guarantees exceeding $11B across all five, and more than a third of FY2027 bits committed (Q3 FY2026 10-Q Note 4; Q3 FY2026 call).
  • Only ~15% of the $41.6B (~$6.2B) is expected to convert to revenue in the next twelve months, against a Q4 FY2026 guidance run-rate of ~$32B/yr — the contracted floor covers roughly a fifth of current revenue, and pricing turns increasingly variable in later contract years (§6, §8).
  • Because volume is fab-capped and cost per bit is set by the node rather than by volume, an extra dollar of price carries almost no extra cost.
  • in FY2023, 77 cents of every lost revenue dollar came out of gross profit, because the 50% fixed-cost obligation and binding purchase orders keep cost flowing while price collapses
  • Gross margin ran 33.3% (FY2022) → 7.1% (FY2023) → 30.1% (FY2025) → 78.4% (Q3 FY2026).
  • Because Q3 FY2026 bits were flat year over year, the COGS comparison is a clean cost-per-bit series independent of the EB estimate: COGS of $1,288M vs $1,313M means cost per gigabyte declined ~2% while price per gigabyte rose 248%.
  • The 4%-of-revenue headline is an artifact of the JV structure plus an exploding revenue denominator, not low capital need (§8, VP3).
  • Top-10 customers were 46% of Q3 FY2026 revenue vs 41% a year earlier (FY2025/24/23: 40%/41%/47%), and one customer exceeded 10% of quarterly revenue — the second consecutive March quarter with a >10% customer (a different customer crossed the threshold in Q3 FY2025); no customer has exceeded 10% in any standalone nine-month or annual period (Q3 FY2026 10-Q Note 3; 10-K).
capitalization
  • The funded debt is gone: the $2.0B spin-off Term Loan B was retired in full on 2026-03-04, about 12.5 months after the spin, and at 2026-04-03 SNDK holds zero debt, $3,735M of cash, and $13,777M of equity (Q3 FY2026 10-Q).
  • The capital-structure risk has migrated entirely to the off-balance-sheet Flash Ventures complex — now extended to 2034 — and to the commitment stack, which grew while the debt shrank: total known material cash requirements rose from $10,308M at FYE2025 to $12,793M at Q3 FY2026 even though the older figure included $2,805M of debt principal and interest.
competitive-advantage
  • Sandisk's advantage is real but contestable, and most of what looks like advantage today is industry-level rather than firm-level.
  • every NAND and DRAM producer's margins exploded in the same two quarters - Micron printed an 84.6% GAAP gross margin (revenue +346% YoY) in its May-2026 quarter, SK hynix 79.3% (revenue +198%), Kioxia 64.1%, Sandisk 78.4% - and top-5 NAND revenue rose 83.7% in one quarter
  • A margin every competitor achieves simultaneously is evidence of industry scarcity, not a company moat.
  • The standalone record fails the durability test - returns below any reasonable cost of capital in three of the last four fiscal years - and the structure that produces the amplitude is contractual: half of Flash Ventures' fixed costs are payable regardless of output.
  • the deck version quantifies it as industry flash capex per percentage point of petabyte-output growth running 1.44x-2.41x the Sandisk+Kioxia level from 2018 to 2024, a widening gap
  • Sandisk's deepest structural weakness is that its key supplier is also its closest competitor: it is barred from fabbing outside Flash Ventures, pays half the JV's fixed costs at any volume, recognizes its 49.9% JV interest one quarter in arrears, and - unlike all three integrated peers - has no DRAM business to rotate toward when NAND is the weaker market (the ~$1B Nanya stake is supply access, not a P&L hedge).
  • On aligned calendar-Q1-2026 quarters the sign flips: SNDK 78.4% vs Kioxia 64.1%, a +14.3-point gross-margin premium on identical wafer economics, worth roughly $850M of quarterly gross profit at SNDK's revenue, on near-identical NAND revenue ($5.95B vs $5.96B per TrendForce [web]).
  • Micron's take-or-pay SCAs (~$5B disclosed RPO with price floors) landed within one quarter of SNDK's NBMs; a mechanism every producer adopts becomes industry structure, not firm-specific advantage.
competitive-landscape
  • Six branded producers on five fab bases: Samsung, SK hynix (including Solidigm), Micron, YMTC, and the shared Kioxia/Sandisk Flash Ventures complex.
  • The Flash Ventures megasite is 80% JV capacity (split 50/50) and 20% Kioxia-only, so Sandisk has access to ~40% of complex output and Kioxia ~60% (Investor Day 2025-02-10, CFO).
  • NAND "TAM" numbers are cycle artifacts — the same market printed $37B and ~$171B annualized within eleven quarters — so volume is the durable sizing basis: industry bit demand at a mid-to-high-teens CAGR, with CY2026 datacenter exabyte growth revised up from ~mid-20s% to "mid-70s%" across four consecutive calls (Q3 FY2026 call).
  • SNDK is the smallest of the five eSSD players and the fastest-growing — share roughly doubled in two quarters on TLC compute-drive qualification wins, with the QLC Stargate product only reaching first revenue in Q4 FY2026.
  • At aligned calendar quarters, SanDisk generated the same NAND revenue as Kioxia ($5.95B vs $5.96B, 1Q26 [web]) from ~40% of the shared complex output versus Kioxia's ~60% — roughly 1.5x the revenue per unit of allocated capacity [estimate: (5.95/40)/(5.96/60); conservative, since SNDK deliberately under-shipped bits in the quarter].
  • SNDK's Q3 FY2026 bits were flat YoY against industry supply growing ~17%, a ~1–2pp bit-share loss on a ~13% base [estimate]; the revenue-share gain is price and mix, not units, and management's stated goal is to keep share, not gain it.
  • Revenue +445% YoY in 1Q CY2026, share reported rising from 8% to 13%, targeting ~15% of global NAND supply by late 2026 on ~150K wafer starts/month with >50% domestic tooling and two further 100K-wpm Wuhan fabs planned
critical-points
  • BEst models 83.1%/82.5% gross margin for FY2027/FY2028 and $48.1B/$54.8B of revenue; the 17-year Micron series never printed an annual gross margin above 58.9%, never repeated a year above 50%, and never produced two consecutive triple-digit growth years; every post-peak year-one reversion ran −13 to −54 gross-margin points (§14).
expectations
  • management guides one quarter at a time and has withdrawn its through-cycle model entirely, while the street's central case models gross margins above 80% for two more years — a level the reference class has never printed for even one annual period — and the base rates place both the consensus growth path and the consensus margin path outside the realized historical distribution
  • the 35% gross-margin model was disowned in January 2026 ("clearly, that's not where we want to be") and no replacement has been issued through the latest event (2026-06-09)
m-and-a
  • The flash business's deal record across three ownership eras is that of a serially disappointing acquirer whose one giant deal was rescued by a commodity cycle rather than by deal execution.
  • WDC's Flash reporting unit ($5,713-5,716M of goodwill) passed every impairment test FY2016-FY2025, with 20% fair-value headroom at the FY2023 trough; the SNDK carve-out - which attributed roughly $2.2B more goodwill by deal ancestry - failed twice in the same window.
  • the tax matters agreement restricts stock issuance, mergers and being acquired through ~February 2027, and the JV agreements' transfer restrictions function as a standing anti-takeover device - any future NAND consolidation must clear SK hynix's position in Kioxia's ownership, the same obstacle that killed it twice
management
  • The standalone sequence executed the stated Investor Day ladder in order and early: net cash reached in Q1 FY2026 (~6 months ahead of target), the term loan retired in full by 2026-03-04, and the $6B buyback authorized 2026-04-30 — after a ~37x move off the April-2025 low close, with zero dollars executed on the filing record through 2026-07-09 (the only post-authorization 8-K is a mini-tender rejection; first actuals arrive with the FY2026 10-K).
market-pricing
  • The move decomposes almost entirely into earnings revisions: the 12-month-forward consensus EPS went from $1.96 (Feb-2025) to $194.76 (Jun-2026) — a ~99x increase — while the forward P/E spent the whole period in a 6.2x–23.9x band (median 9.4x, 11.7x at June-30-2026).
risks
  • Gross profit moves almost one-for-one with ASP: wafer cost is cost-plus, half of JV fixed costs are payable at any volume, so price moves drop nearly fully to gross profit in both directions.
secular-trends
  • Datacenter demand is AI-inference storage (KV-cache, retrieval corpora) plus ordinary cloud growth: management's CY2026 industry datacenter bit-growth estimate was revised up three times in three quarters — mid-20s% → mid-40s% → 60s% → "mid-70s%" (Q1–Q3 FY2026 calls) — and its KV-cache estimate alone is 75–100 incremental exabytes in CY2027, doubling the year after (Q2 FY2026 call; management estimate, not externally corroborated).
  • node-to-node cost-per-bit reductions slowed from ~24%/yr across the 2D generations to ~11%/yr across the 3D generations [deck: slide 19], so buyers now get about half the annual price decline they used to. This is the one pricing argument that does not depend on the cycle
  • The realized five-year bit CAGR (FY2021→FY2026) is ~12.5% [estimate: chained disclosed changes, bounds 11.5–13.5%] — below the mid-to-high-teens plan management invests to; the plan is a target, not a track record.
  • TrendForce forecasts 3Q26 NAND contract prices +10–15% QoQ, decelerating from +70–75% in 2Q26 and +55–60% in 1Q26, because "price tolerance among consumer customers has reached its limit"
  • The NBM structure is real, large, and first-of-its-kind at this scale: five agreements, $41.6B filed RPO on three of them, >$11B guarantees (call-only figure; the 10-Q shows only the $0.4B prepayment portion on the balance sheet), >1/3 of FY2027 bits committed with a >50% target.
  • the CFO's assertion that even floor prices deliver margins "consistent with the margins that we guided... for our fiscal fourth quarter" (~79–81% GM) is the most aggressive unverifiable claim in the record — no disclosure permits checking it
technology
  • The 3D NAND process and memory design (BiCS) are co-developed and jointly owned with Kioxia — "We and Kioxia jointly own these co-developed flash technologies" (10-K FY2025, Item 1) — and Kioxia sells the same die into the same markets.
  • BiCS8 is a 218-layer node built with CBA ("CMOS directly Bonded to Array"): the control logic and the memory array are fabricated on separate wafers, each with its own optimized process, then bonded face to face.
  • That is the lowest in-production layer count of the four scaled NAND makers — SK hynix ships 321 layers, Samsung 286, Micron 276.
  • Delivery so far is on schedule: BiCS8 went from ~7% of bits (Q4 FY2025) to 15% (Q1 FY2026), guided to the majority of bit production exiting FY2026, qualified across major PC OEMs (Q4 FY2025 and Q1 FY2026 calls).
  • SNDK designs controllers in-house and fabs them at third-party foundries — outside the Kioxia JV, the one technology layer where the twins diverge (10-K, Item 1).
  • The switching costs are structural: "Building an ASIC is not easy... getting qualified at a major customer can be a 2-year process" with 3-4-year design cycles (CEO, Mizuho 2026-06-09).
  • HBF re-architects a NAND die into many parallel-accessed sub-arrays, then stacks 16 die with a proprietary low-warpage process into an HBM-like package: matching HBM read bandwidth with 8-16x the capacity at similar cost, for holding model weights and KV-cache in AI inference.
  • Management itself has narrowed the pitch: HBF is "not a substitute for DRAM" and won't "take over for enterprise SSD" (CEO, Bernstein SDC 2026-05-28) — it works where reads are large, sequential and predictable, because NAND random-read latency is orders of magnitude slower than DRAM.
value-chain
  • Upcycle: supply can grow only mid-to-high teens via nodal transitions while management's own datacenter demand view runs mid-70s% for CY2026; fabs are full; ~110-day wafer cycle times and 2-year qualification cycles mean spending more cannot add supply quickly; and the non-compete bars SNDK from adding non-JV supply. The structure that caps SNDK's bits also caps the industry's.
  • Through FY2023-FY2025 the equipment layer held 47-49% gross margins every year while the producer layer ran GAAP losses; in FY2026 the producer layer repriced ~50 points in three quarters against an equipment layer that moved ~1 point — the profit pool moved decisively to the producers for the first time in the five-year window.
  • Against Kioxia, the cash flows and asset ownership put the leverage with the fab owner: SNDK pays $1.2B for continued access, prepays Kioxia's building depreciation ($856M outstanding + $373M committed), guarantees $993M of JV equipment leases, and paid $3.4B/yr for wafers and net loans against $176M of cumulative distributions received over three years — there is no disclosed reciprocal payment from Kioxia.
variant-perceptions
  • The contract arithmetic says the NBMs floor volume, not margin.
  • The capital sits in the JV and in commitments, not in PP&E: gross capex including the JV share averaged 16% of revenue pre-spin [deck: Investor Day, slide 76], Kioxia spends 12.0% of revenue against the same fab base, committed FV-related outlays were $2,226M for FY2026 alone versus ~$960M of annualized "gross capex", and the JV tool bill arrives through future wafer prices.
  • Blended revenue share of ~13.9% merely returns to WDC-flash's ~14% of 2020; the 1Q26 uptick came in a quarter when SNDK's own bits declined high-teens sequentially - bit share declined while price and mix rose; Samsung gained more (+3.6pp QoQ).
