1. Business description, model & unit economics

What DKNG sells and how it reports

DraftKings sells regulated digital gambling and adjacent real-money products to consumers. The company reports one consolidated operating segment, but revenue is disaggregated into Sportsbook, iGaming, and Other. FY2025 revenue was $6.055bn, split $3.827bn Sportsbook, $1.805bn iGaming, and $422.8mm Other; Q1 2026 revenue was $1.646bn, split $1.095bn Sportsbook, $461.3mm iGaming, and $89.9mm Other. [business-model--sportsbook.md; business-model--igaming.md; business-model--other.md; financial-historian.md]

The customer journey begins with identity, geolocation, payment rails, responsible-gaming checks, and a funded wallet. From there the same user can move among products that look similar in the app but have different accounting and risk profiles. Sportsbook is a fixed-odds risk business: DraftKings prices an event, accepts the bet, and keeps the net spread after payouts, promotions, and settlement. iGaming is a casino-content business: a player repeatedly wagers into house-banked games where game math, content mix, jackpot design, and supplier terms drive economics. DFS/Pick6 is more rake-like, Lottery is a courier/commission model, deposit interest is a balance-sheet float item, and Predictions is closer to exchange/broker/market-making economics than to a state-licensed Sportsbook wager. [business-model--sportsbook.md; business-model--igaming.md; business-model--other.md; technology.md]

That product map matters because the consolidated income statement blends at least four different economic models. A Sportsbook dollar of handle is mostly customer payout with a mid-single/high-single-cent net revenue capture. A casino dollar of handle is much smaller disclosed revenue yield but much higher frequency, and the profit split depends on proprietary versus licensed content. A Lottery or DFS dollar is not comparable to handle at all because ticket face value, contest prizes, and fees are recognized differently. A Predictions dollar of traded volume is not revenue until the fee, spread, or market-making economics are disclosed. The right underwriting unit is therefore product-specific; consolidated revenue growth alone can hide whether the mix is improving unit economics or merely adding lower-visibility volume. [business-model--sportsbook.md; business-model--igaming.md; business-model--other.md; value-chain.md]

The IR proxy is a "$40bn+ TAM" and an "OSB adoption flywheel," but the ultimate exposure decomposes to states-live × adult population × payers-per-capita × handle-per-payer × structural hold × (1 − promo). FY2022-25 revenue nearly tripled mostly on payer count (MUPs 1.9m → 4.0m, +110%) added by a finite new-state legalization S-curve and a half-cyclical hold climb (NRM 4.4% → 7.1%), not on durable same-state deepening (ARPMUP $96 → $125, +30%). The largest remaining OSB launch population (CA, TX, FL) is politically stuck. The new-state launches that produced most of the growth are one-off and nearly finished (§1, §6, §12).

The owner can see consolidated profitability improving but cannot see which product line created each dollar of incremental profit. [business-model--sportsbook.md; completeness-critic.md]

Sportsbook money flow

Sportsbook is a fixed-odds wagering product. The customer places a wager at odds set by DraftKings, the event settles, the customer is paid if the wager wins, and DraftKings records revenue from the wager economics after payouts, promotions, and incentives. The reported equation is Sportsbook revenue equals settled Sportsbook handle multiplied by Sportsbook net revenue margin. [business-model--sportsbook.md]

The FY2025 Sportsbook bridge was more monetization than volume. Handle grew from $48.061bn in FY2024 to $53.554bn in FY2025, while Sportsbook revenue grew from $2.903bn to $3.827bn and exact NRM rose from 6.04% to 7.15%. Roughly $592mm of the $924mm incremental Sportsbook revenue came from NRM expansion and about $332mm came from handle growth [estimate: price/volume bridge from disclosed handle and revenue]. Q1 2026 sharpened that pattern: handle grew only 1.5% year over year to $14.083bn, while revenue grew 24.1% to $1.095bn because NRM rose to 7.8%. [business-model--sportsbook.md; phase3_adversarial_summary.json]

iGaming money flow

iGaming is online casino. The customer plays blackjack, roulette, baccarat, slots, or other casino games where DraftKings acts like the house, and revenue is wagers net of payouts and incentives when the game or hand concludes. DraftKings offered iGaming in Connecticut, Michigan, New Jersey, Pennsylvania, West Virginia, and Ontario as of March 31, 2026, so the first gate is state authorization rather than national consumer demand. [business-model--igaming.md]

The best disclosed product anatomy is the split between proprietary casino content, supplier-licensed content, and embedded Sportsbook-app distribution. DraftKings said in-house studio content produced $30bn+ of 2025 handle, 36% of total Casino handle, and $550mm of in-house studio GGR; the same deck said embedded Casino inside the Sportsbook app represented 62% of total Casino handle. These facts support a real product-control effort, but also show that roughly 64% of Casino handle came from content outside the in-house studio and that supplier revenue-share rates remain undisclosed. [business-model--igaming.md; deck-analyst.md]

Other and Predictions

Other is not one business. It includes DFS/Fantasy and Pick6 contest rake, Lottery courier service fees and commissions through Jackpocket, Prediction Markets economics, and customer-deposit interest. FY2025 Other revenue was $422.8mm, including $25.6mm of customer-deposit interest, so non-interest Other product revenue was $397.2mm [estimate: arithmetic]. Q1 2026 Other revenue declined 13.0% year over year to $89.9mm because of lower Fantasy entry fees and lower Lottery revenue after the Texas exit. [business-model--other.md; phase3_adversarial_summary.json]

Prediction Markets launched on December 19, 2025 and uses a different economic stack from state-licensed Sportsbook. DraftKings acquired Railbird Exchange as a CFTC-licensed DCM foundation, disclosed April annualized Predictions consumer volume above $1bn and total traded volume above $2.3bn, and said market making had a positive return. The missing pieces are the revenue take rate, market-making P&L, clearing and FCM costs, CAC payback, customer overlap with Sportsbook, and regulatory durability. Operating leverage and capital intensity

The business is capital-light in physical assets and capital-heavy in customer acquisition, technology, licenses, and regulation. FY2025 PP&E capex plus capitalized software was $146.5mm, or 2.4% of revenue, while sales and marketing was $1.380bn, or 22.8% of revenue. 2. Key technologies & technical differentiators

Sportsbook pricing, trading, and live stack

DraftKings' most important technology is the Sportsbook trading system: odds creation, risk controls, live-market availability, same-game parlay construction, bet acceptance, settlement, and payout speed. Investor Day 2026 cited 95% in-house content, 100,000 simulations, 84% live mainline availability, 517 live betting options per game, 92% live bet success, and 89% of payouts under 60 seconds. These figures matter because they can raise parlay depth, improve wager success, reduce abandoned bets, and support net revenue margin. [technology.md; deck-analyst.md]

The replication difficulty is not one algorithm. It is the combination of data feeds, trading models, risk limits, product UI, settlement operations, and a large enough customer base to learn from live activity. The substitution threat is that FanDuel, BetMGM, Caesars, Fanatics, and specialist suppliers can develop or acquire pieces of the stack, while exchange-based event contracts can shift some users from bookmaker pricing to order-book liquidity. [technology.md; competitive-landscape--sportsbook.md]

Data science and AI operating layer

DraftKings uses data science inside promotions, risk, trading, customer support, fraud, engineering, and personalization. Management cited automated and personalized promotional spend, a 1,300 bps NRM improvement on promotional wagers, roughly $45mm of annualized machine-learning-driven gross-revenue uplift, 40% engineering-hour productivity, 25% chatbot containment, and a 0.15% chargeback rate. [technology.md; phase3_adversarial_summary.json]

Better models can reduce wasted promotions, accept more good bets, reject more bad ones, shorten support interactions, and make cross-channel conversion more relevant. The risk is that the same tools are available to other scaled operators and to suppliers, so AI should be treated as an operating lever inside a scale platform, not a standalone moat. [technology.md; risks.md]

Wallet, identity, compliance, and Super App

The unified wallet lets DraftKings move a known, funded user across Sportsbook, Casino, Fantasy, Lottery, and Predictions inside one identity and payments environment. Management said the Super App reaches roughly 80%-90% of 11mm customers each year, and the Q1 2026 deck said the Predictions product was embedded into the Super App with 69% of sports Predictions consumer volume from non-sportsbook states in the early period. [technology.md; deck-analyst.md]

This layer lowers friction and can raise customer lifetime value, but it is not exclusive. Other national operators also have wallets, loyalty systems, and compliance stacks. DraftKings' edge is strongest when the wallet is tied to product depth and customer data that make the next wager, casino session, or event contract easier to place without overpaying for the user. [technology.md; competitive-advantage--other.md]

iGaming studio and supplier dependency

The iGaming stack includes in-house casino games, dynamic jackpots, daily rewards, Rocket mechanics, original titles, embedded casino journeys, and supplier-licensed games from IGT, Light & Wonder, Spin, Evolution, and others. Self-developed blackjack and other major games can retain more revenue than licensed supplier games, but DraftKings still depends on third-party content for most Casino handle. [technology.md; business-model--igaming.md]

The technical question is whether proprietary studio mix can lift gross margin and retention without narrowing the game catalog. FY2025 in-house studio GGR of $550mm is material, but it is not the same as product revenue, gross profit, or segment cash flow. The product edge is promising and measurable; the economic edge is not yet fully disclosed. [business-model--igaming.md; competitive-advantage--igaming.md]

Predictions and Railbird stack

Predictions uses broker, FCM, DCM, clearinghouse, market-making, and Super App distribution layers. DraftKings owns Railbird Exchange, has disclosed a road map involving Crypto.com, CME, market making, FCM, DCM, and DCO components, and expects adjusted gross margin 10 to 30 percentage points higher than Sportsbook if the product avoids state gaming taxes and long-term promotions. [technology.md; deck-analyst.md; business-model--other.md]

The bottleneck is not only software. CFTC/NFA oversight, state gaming reaction, exchange liquidity, professional market-maker share, clearing economics, and event-contract litigation determine whether the stack becomes a high-margin national product or a costly option. The 2026 investment plan of $200mm-$300mm is large enough to matter before steady-state unit economics are visible. [risks.md; assumption-refuter.md]

Substitution threats

The main substitutes are not static. In Sportsbook, a user can choose FanDuel, BetMGM, Caesars, Fanatics, ESPN Bet/Penn, Underdog, PrizePicks, offshore operators, DFS, or no bet. In iGaming, a player can choose FanDuel, BetMGM, Caesars, Rush Street, supplier-branded content through other casinos, sweepstakes products, or land-based casinos. In Predictions, a trader can use Kalshi-like event-contract venues, Crypto.com-style venues, other FCM/DCM combinations, or traditional Sportsbook if regulators narrow event contracts. [competitive-landscape--sportsbook.md; competitive-landscape--igaming.md; competitive-landscape--other.md]

3. Competitive advantages & moat assessment

DraftKings' moat is not one moat. It is a bundle of product-specific advantages: scale and trading depth in Sportsbook, wallet and content control in iGaming, and optionality in adjacent regulated products. The bundle is valuable only if it converts into better revenue per wager, lower promotional waste, higher retained gross profit, and eventually lower sales-and-marketing intensity. That is why the most useful moat test is not brand awareness by itself; it is whether the company can show improving unit economics while direct competitors with similar licenses and products are trying to copy every profitable feature. [competitive-advantage--sportsbook.md; competitive-advantage--igaming.md; competitive-advantage--other.md]

Sportsbook advantage

DraftKings has a real Sportsbook advantage, but it is not unchallenged. The evidence is observable in scale, brand, product depth, in-house trading, parlay/live tools, and rising Sportsbook NRM from 4.4% in FY2022 to 7.1% in FY2025 and 7.8% in Q1 2026. The advantage comes from several pieces that reinforce one another: a national app and wallet, enough handle to learn from pricing and promotion experiments, trading tools that support more live markets and same-game parlay depth, and a customer base that can be cross-promoted into adjacent products. [competitive-advantage--sportsbook.md; technology.md; business-model--sportsbook.md]

For the owner, the durable edge is not the 7.8% Q1 2026 NRM by itself. The edge is proven only if DraftKings can keep more revenue per handle while maintaining handle growth, MUP quality, and marketing efficiency. A truly stronger Sportsbook franchise would show high NRM, healthy handle growth, and lower acquisition intensity at the same time. [business-model--sportsbook.md; assumption-refuter.md]

iGaming advantage

The iGaming advantage is less settled. DraftKings has embedded Sportsbook-to-Casino distribution, proprietary content, a large wallet, and management's claim of second-highest iGaming share. Against that, Q1 2026 iGaming revenue grew only 8.9%, declined sequentially from Q4 2025, and management acknowledged that online casino growth had lagged the market after too much emphasis on OSB conversion rather than iCasino-first and slots-first users. That admission matters because iGaming is not just Sportsbook monetization with a casino tab attached. The casino user can be slot-first, jackpot-first, dealer-game-first, rewards-first, or entertainment-first, and the acquisition funnel can differ materially from sports betting. [competitive-advantage--igaming.md; business-model--igaming.md]

The proprietary studio is the strongest iGaming evidence because it connects product control to potential retained revenue. DraftKings disclosed $30bn+ of in-house studio handle, 36% of total Casino handle, and $550mm of in-house studio GGR in 2025. That is large enough to matter, but it also means roughly 64% of Casino handle still came through supplier or non-studio content. The product control is real; whether it earns a return is unproved, because DraftKings discloses no supplier revenue share, game-level margin, casino-only CAC, casino-only retention, or iGaming gross profit. [business-model--igaming.md; competitive-advantage--igaming.md; deck-analyst.md]

Other and Predictions advantage

Other has distribution assets but not yet a proven economic moat. DFS/Fantasy, Lottery, Predictions, and deposit interest have different customer events, regulatory regimes, revenue recognition, and cost pools. Predictions has early volume, a Railbird infrastructure path, Super App distribution, and market-making commentary, but Other revenue declined 13.0% in Q1 2026 and Prediction Markets revenue is not separately disclosed. The clearest advantage is launch capability: DraftKings can push a new regulated product into a funded, verified, high-intent customer base. The clearest weakness is that launch capability is not the same as profitable demand. [competitive-advantage--other.md; business-model--other.md]

Predictions could become a moat if the product creates a national event-contract layer with lower state-tax leakage, meaningful non-sportsbook-state volume, and repeat activity that does not cannibalize the core book. It could also become an expensive regulatory option if state gaming regulators, tribes, leagues, professional-market-maker economics, or exchange liquidity capture the upside. The current evidence is launch, volume, and infrastructure; it is not revenue, take rate, market-making P&L, or CAC payback. FY2027-FY2030 Other revenue CAGR in the base model is refuted by the available evidence until Predictions revenue economics are disclosed. [assumption-refuter.md; risks.md; competitive-landscape--other.md]

What would prove or expose the moat

The advantage strengthens if four things appear together: Sportsbook NRM stays near or above 7.5% without handle stagnation; iGaming growth reaccelerates toward market growth without a large promotion increase; sales and marketing declines as a percentage of revenue after Predictions investment; and diluted share count begins to decline after SBC and buybacks. It weakens if higher NRM coincides with lower handle, iGaming remains below market, Predictions revenue is small relative to investment, or repurchases only offset dilution. [risks.md; modeler.md; capitalization.md]

DKNG has enough product depth and scale to be a durable leader, but the best evidence still sits in Sportsbook NRM and consolidated operating leverage. The iGaming and Predictions claims need product-level proof. The next two years should make that visible through three observable markers: whether iGaming catches up to market growth, whether Predictions produces disclosed revenue instead of only volume, and whether the company can lower marketing intensity while preserving MUP and ARPMUP growth. 4. Competitive landscape & market sizing

Market structure and addressable pool

DraftKings competes in overlapping but economically different markets: U.S. online sports betting, regulated online casino, DFS/pick'em sports entertainment, lottery courier, and emerging event-contract prediction markets. The total addressable-market number therefore depends heavily on what is included. AGA reported 2025 U.S. commercial sports-betting revenue of $16.96bn and iGaming revenue of $10.74bn, or $27.70bn combined. DraftKings' own 2025 TAM framing is larger because it includes different scopes, potential state expansion, and adjacent products. The useful owner view is to separate the lines: Sportsbook is already large and concentrated; iGaming is smaller but high frequency and state-constrained; Other is a portfolio of smaller or less disclosed businesses; Predictions is a regulatory option with early volume but no revenue disclosure. [competitive-landscape--sportsbook.md; competitive-landscape--igaming.md; competitive-landscape--other.md; secular-trends.md]

5. Value chain & profit pools

Sportsbook handle dollar versus revenue dollar

A $1 Sportsbook handle view shows why scale and margin both matter. In FY2025, $1 of DraftKings Sportsbook handle became about 7.15 cents of Sportsbook revenue. The rest went back to customers through payouts or was reduced through wager economics and promotional mechanics. That means a 100 bp change in NRM on more than $50bn of handle can matter more than several points of handle growth, which is exactly what happened in FY2025 and Q1 2026. [value-chain.md; business-model--sportsbook.md]

The revenue-dollar view is different. Once the 7.15 cents of revenue exists, external and internal profit pools take their share: state gaming taxes, market-access partners, payment processors, data suppliers, technology infrastructure, customer acquisition, product development, compliance, and corporate overhead. In FY2025, each $1 of DraftKings revenue included about $0.349 of gaming taxes, $0.197 of other adjusted cost of revenue, and $0.455 of adjusted gross profit. It then used about $0.221 for adjusted sales and marketing, $0.061 for product and technology, and $0.070 for G&A. This is a consolidated view, not a product P&L, but it identifies the largest economic claims on the revenue dollar. [value-chain.md; financial-historian.md]

Tax, market-access, and promotion leakage

Gaming taxes are the largest visible external leakage. Q1 2026 gaming taxes were $569.5mm, or 34.6 cents per revenue dollar, while cost of revenue was 57.7% of revenue. State-level tax rates and market-access terms can change not only margin, but also promotion strategy: DraftKings disclosed FY2025 promotions at 2.4% of handle in sportsbook-only states with tax rates above 30%, versus 4.2% in states below 30%. High tax can force lower promotions, worse customer value, or lower operator margin; low tax can support better reinvestment and a healthier customer funnel. [value-chain.md; business-model--sportsbook.md]

Market access is a bargaining-power issue because many states require casino, tribe, or track relationships. DraftKings can absorb or renegotiate some costs as a scaled operator, but cannot unilaterally remove state taxes or local license constraints. The failed 2024 high-tax-state surcharge episode shows that customers and competitors can limit tax pass-through: even when the economic logic is clear, the market may not allow the operator to explicitly charge users for state tax leakage. [secular-trends.md; risks.md; phase3_adversarial_summary.json]

iGaming supplier and content profit pools

iGaming has a different value chain from Sportsbook. The customer still uses the DraftKings wallet, but the revenue dollar passes through game math, proprietary versus third-party content, jackpot mechanics, live dealer/studio capacity, platform integrations, and state casino taxes. DraftKings can improve economics by owning more content, routing users toward proprietary games, and designing rewards that lift retention without pure promotional subsidy. But content breadth still matters, and a casino catalog without supplier titles may be less competitive. [business-model--igaming.md; technology.md; competitive-advantage--igaming.md]

iGaming suppliers retain profit pools where DraftKings does not own content. Light & Wonder is the closest public supplier comparison, with FY2025 iGaming revenue of $337mm and AEBITDA of $125mm, or a 37.1% margin, and Q1 2026 AEBITDA margin of 36%. Evolution, IGT, Light & Wonder, and other suppliers can capture economics through content fees, platform relationships, and live-dealer infrastructure. That supports the existence of supplier economics, but it does not prove Light & Wonder is the perfect proxy for every supplier or every casino game. [value-chain.md; phase3_adversarial_summary.json]

Predictions value chain

Predictions suppliers and counterparties are different: FCM, DCM, DCO, exchange technology, clearing, market makers, professional liquidity, regulators, and legal/compliance advisors. If the product avoids state gaming taxes and relies on federal event-contract rails, the adjusted gross margin could be higher than Sportsbook. But that thesis depends on the economics not being captured by clearing, venue, liquidity, legal, or customer-acquisition costs. It also depends on regulators allowing the product to remain broad enough to matter. [technology.md; business-model--other.md; risks.md]

DraftKings has disclosed early annualized consumer and total trading volume for Predictions, but volume is not revenue. The missing line items are the customer fee or spread, market-making P&L, external venue or clearing cost, FCM cost, liquidity-provider economics, tax treatment, and incremental marketing. A lower-tax product can still be a poor profit pool if liquidity and compliance costs are high or if professional traders capture most of the economics. [competitive-landscape--other.md; assumption-refuter.md]

Media, distribution, and infrastructure commitments

Media and partnership commitments are material. DraftKings had $1.3bn of expected obligations over five years from three media counterparties signed in Q3 2025. These commitments can lower customer acquisition cost if they deliver high-quality traffic, but they are fixed enough to matter in a lower-growth or higher-tax scenario. The strategic question is whether media spend produces funded, repeat, multi-product users rather than one-time promo-seeking bettors. [value-chain.md; capitalization.md]

Mobile app stores, cloud providers, payment processors, sports-data vendors, and compliance vendors are necessary infrastructure. The workpapers do not show app stores taking direct core wagering revenue share, but platform availability, payments, chargebacks, data reliability, geolocation, KYC, and cloud uptime still affect product quality and regulatory standing. Payment friction, failed deposits, chargebacks, or slow withdrawals can damage retention even when odds and promotions are competitive. [technology.md; risks.md]

Bargaining-power trajectory

DraftKings' bargaining power rises with scale against media partners, payment vendors, data suppliers, and some market-access partners, but falls where the state controls the license, tax rate, or legal product definition. The profit-pool question is therefore jurisdiction-specific: a low-tax, mature state with strong parlay/live adoption can look attractive; a high-tax state with costly access and heavy promotions can consume most of the revenue dollar. [value-chain.md; risks.md]

The biggest upside to the value chain is operating leverage: once customers, licenses, wallet, risk systems, and compliance infrastructure are in place, incremental wager or casino activity can carry high contribution if promotional and tax leakage are controlled. The biggest downside is that every new growth vector can reopen a cost line. iGaming needs content and casino-specific acquisition. Predictions needs legal, liquidity, exchange, and market-making infrastructure. New states need launches, licensing, market access, and promotion. DKNG's business quality will be determined by whether scale lowers those costs as a percentage of revenue faster than the next growth vector adds them. [financial-historian.md; modeler.md; assumption-refuter.md]

6. Secular & industry trends

The first secular trend is legal access. DraftKings Sportsbook was live in most major legal online sports betting jurisdictions and iGaming was live in a narrower set of states plus Ontario. Sportsbook legalization has spread faster than iGaming, while iGaming remains constrained by state politics and casino stakeholder negotiations. A legal-access expansion remains the cleanest TAM lever, but the timing is uncertain and state tax rates can alter profitability. [secular-trends.md; risks.md]

The second trend is monetization, not just handle. DraftKings Sportsbook handle grew from $37.4bn in FY2023 to $48.1bn in FY2024 and $53.6bn in FY2025. AGA U.S. legal sports handle grew from $121.06bn in 2023 to $149.90bn in 2024 and $166.94bn in 2025. DraftKings' rough handle share moved from 30.9% to 32.1% to 32.1%, suggesting the visible share gain was mostly 2024, while FY2025 revenue growth came more from net revenue margin. [secular-trends.md; phase3_adversarial_summary.json]

The third trend is product depth. Parlay mix, live betting, personalized promotions, faster settlement, and better risk tools can lift revenue per handle and customer engagement. The risk is that higher sports outcome margin, promo cuts, and handle can move together. Management explicitly said after the 2025 guidance miss that assumptions around handle, parlay-driven margin, and promotional reductions interacted more than expected. [risks.md; phase3_adversarial_summary.json]

iGaming remains a high-growth regulated market. AGA reported 2025 U.S. commercial iGaming revenue of $10.74bn, up 27.6%, and April 2026 iGaming revenue of $1.00bn, up 15.0%. DraftKings' iGaming revenue grew at a 21.8% CAGR from FY2023 to FY2025, but its rough DKNG/AGA ratio moved from about 19.7% in 2023 to 16.8% in 2025 on the workpaper's denominator, and management acknowledged recent market lag. The ratio is directional because DKNG includes Ontario and AGA is U.S. commercial data. [secular-trends.md; competitive-landscape--igaming.md]

Prediction Markets could alter the regulatory slope. If federally regulated sports event contracts remain available, DraftKings can reach customers outside traditional sportsbook states and potentially avoid state gaming taxes. If state regulators, tribes, leagues, or courts constrain event contracts, Predictions may remain an investment burden or a niche product. The current evidence is launch, volume, and infrastructure, not steady-state economics. [business-model--other.md; risks.md; competitive-landscape--other.md]

7. Critical points

The first critical point is the durability of Sportsbook net revenue margin. FY2025 net revenue margin rose to 7.1%, Q1 2026 reached 7.8%, and product work supports the direction. But DraftKings has not yet sustained an 8.0% annual Sportsbook net revenue margin, and sports outcomes have been large enough to move annual guidance. The model gives partial credit for structural improvement but treats FY2030 8.0% as a favorable assumption rather than a proven steady-state. [assumption-refuter.md; risks.md]

The second critical point is whether iGaming can reaccelerate. Online casino can be a higher-quality revenue stream than seasonal sports wagering, yet Q1 2026 iGaming growth slowed to 8.9% while management admitted that the product focus had lagged. The company has proprietary content and embedded distribution, but the absence of iGaming handle, supplier cost, customer cohort, and margin data limits confidence. [business-model--igaming.md; competitive-advantage--igaming.md]

The third critical point is Predictions. Management framed Predictions as a large opportunity with an adjusted gross margin 10 to 30 percentage points higher than Sportsbook and broad national reach. The audited evidence today is early volume, positive market-making commentary, Railbird infrastructure, and a $200mm-$300mm FY2026 investment plan. Without revenue, take rate, market-making P&L, CAC payback, customer overlap, and regulatory outcomes, the product is a major swing factor but not an established profit pool. [business-model--other.md; assumption-refuter.md]

The fourth critical point is capital allocation per share. FY2024-FY2025 marked a cash-generation inflection, with about $1.08bn of operating cash flow, but cash deployment into PP&E/software/licenses, acquisitions, program repurchases, and RSU/tax withholding totaled about $1.71bn before debt financing and working-capital items. FY2025 repurchases did not prevent diluted share count from rising. The shareholder test is not only total FCF, but FCF per share after SBC, acquisitions, and refinancing. [capital-allocation.md; capitalization.md; phase3_adversarial_summary.json]

The fifth critical point is governance. Jason Robins remains co-founder, CEO, chair, and controlling holder with 88.3% voting power as of the 2026 proxy. Founder alignment and continuity are meaningful, and named executive officers have heavy equity exposure. Minority holders also have limited ability to influence strategy, incentives, or capital allocation if priorities diverge. [management-incentives.md]

8. Variant perceptions

The constructive variant perception is that DraftKings is emerging from investment mode into a scale platform. The evidence: FY2022 adjusted EBITDA of -$721.8mm became FY2025 adjusted EBITDA of +$620.0mm; operating cash flow moved from -$625.5mm to +$662.9mm; Sportsbook net revenue margin expanded; product and technology ratios declined; and Q1 2026 still grew revenue 16.8% despite only 1.5% Sportsbook handle growth. [financial-historian.md; history-inflections.md]

The cautious variant perception is that the market is extrapolating a cleaner margin path than the operating evidence supports. FY2026 guidance is $700mm-$900mm adjusted EBITDA after Predictions investment, far below the old 2023 Investor Day $1.4bn FY2026 adjusted EBITDA target. Consensus was revised down sharply over the prior year, and the model's base case sits below BEst after FY2026. Sportsbook margin, iGaming reacceleration, sales and marketing leverage, tax burden, SBC, and Other growth all need to cooperate. [expectations-internal.md; expectations-external.md; modeler.md]

Another question is whether Predictions expands the addressable market or cannibalizes Sportsbook. Management disclosed that 98% of sports consumer volume in competitive sportsbook states remained on Sportsbook and that 69% of DraftKings Predictions sports consumer volume came from non-sportsbook states in an early Q1 2026 deck. That is encouraging early incrementality evidence, but the period is short, the product is new, and economics are not disclosed. [deck-analyst.md; competitive-advantage--other.md]

A final question is whether repurchases create per-share discipline or mostly offset equity compensation. DraftKings increased authorization to $2bn and repurchased $571.5mm in FY2025 plus $98.6mm in Q1 2026. Through Q1 2026 it had repurchased 20.4mm shares at an average $35.21, while the 2026-06-23 price was $25.15 and Class A shares outstanding still rose. The per-share outcome remains unresolved. [capital-allocation.md; capitalization.md]

9. History & inflection points

DraftKings was founded in 2011 as a daily fantasy sports business. The strategic pivot toward regulated online sports betting and iGaming accelerated after the 2018 U.S. sports-betting legal opening. The December 23, 2019 announced transaction with Diamond Eagle and SBTech, closed on April 23, 2020, created a public company with a vertically integrated sports-betting technology path. [history-inflections.md]

The FY2020-FY2022 period was a capital-intensive land grab. Revenue grew from $614.5mm in FY2020 to $2.240bn in FY2022, but adjusted EBITDA reached -$721.8mm in FY2022 and operating cash flow was -$625.5mm. The 2021 zero-coupon convertible note raised $1.265bn and remains the primary 2028 maturity wall. [financial-historian.md; capitalization.md]

The 2021-2022 M&A cycle expanded product scope. DraftKings announced Golden Nugget Online Gaming in August 2021 and closed in May 2022; management described at least $300mm of mature EBITDA synergies, but filings said post-close GNOG revenue and earnings were impracticable to determine separately. The company also acquired VSiN in 2021 and sold it in 2024, recording a $5.8mm sale loss. [m-and-a-history.md; phase3_adversarial_summary.json]

The 2023-2025 period was the profitability turn. DraftKings announced operating-cost actions, produced positive Q2 2023 adjusted EBITDA, and delivered FY2025 adjusted EBITDA of $620.0mm. FY2025 revenue was $6.055bn, gross margin was 41.3%, operating loss narrowed to $15.8mm, net income was $3.7mm, operating cash flow was $662.9mm, and free cash flow after capitalized software was $516.3mm. [history-inflections.md; financial-historian.md]

The 2024 high-tax-state surcharge episode showed both tax pressure and competitive limits. DraftKings announced a surcharge as a high-tax-state margin solution, then abandoned it after customer feedback and after FanDuel declined to follow. This matters because taxes are the largest value-chain leakage and a structural state-level risk. [secular-trends.md; risks.md; phase3_adversarial_summary.json]

The latest inflection is Prediction Markets and reporting change. DraftKings acquired Railbird in 2025, launched Predictions in December 2025, and said it would combine Sportsbook and sports Predictions in a new Sports revenue reporting line after Q1 2026. This may make separation of legacy Sportsbook and Predictions harder just as Predictions investment becomes material. [history-inflections.md; business-model--other.md; completeness-critic.md]

Founder control and operating roles

Jason Robins remains co-founder, CEO, chair, and the controlling voting holder. As of the 2026 proxy, he controlled 88.3% of voting power, while directors and executive officers together controlled 88.5%. Incentive plan

Founder NEOs have $1 base salaries, roughly 90% of average NEO compensation is equity-based, and Robins' 2025 compensation was $22.622mm, mostly $19.204mm of stock awards, with no non-equity bonus. Convert and capped-call mechanics

The 2028 convert is the main maturity. The conversion price is $94.85 and the capped-call cap is $135.50, both far above the $25.15 share price on 2026-06-23. At that price, the convert is a refinancing problem rather than an in-the-money equity conversion path. [capitalization.md; phase3_adversarial_summary.json]

Refinancing the $1.265bn convert at the Q1 2026 Term B rate of 5.42% would add about $68.6mm of annual cash interest relative to the zero cash coupon; a 100 bps higher rate would raise the cost to about $81.2mm. The model's FY2028 net interest expense assumption holds because cash balances should earn some interest, but gross refinancing cost remains a real cash drag. [capitalization.md; assumption-refuter.md]

Term Loan and refinancing cost

The Term B Loan is floating-rate secured debt. A 100 bps rate move changes annual pre-tax interest by about $5.9mm on the $594.0mm Q1 2026 principal. The loan is not the maturity wall, but it sets the observable refinancing-rate anchor for the convert and increases the cash-interest burden versus the zero-coupon capital structure. [capitalization.md]

Method note: the weighted-average cash interest sensitivity uses outstanding principal multiplied by the reported rate or rate change. It excludes amortization, fees, and interest income on cash, so it is a gross debt-cost sensitivity rather than net interest expense. [capitalization.md]

Leases, commitments, and contingent obligations

Operating leases stepped up but are not the central fixed-charge issue. FY2025 lease liabilities were $54.2mm present value and $66.9mm undiscounted cash flows; Q1 2026 balance-sheet lease liabilities were $82.5mm. Larger commitments include debt refinancing, media obligations, market-access contracts, surety requirements, and indirect-tax contingencies. [capitalization.md; phase3_adversarial_summary.json]

Other obligations include $2.272bn of non-cancelable vendor contracts, including about $1.3bn of media obligations over five years, and $500mm of surety bonds at a 0.5% annual premium. Q1 2026 indirect-tax contingent liability was $92.1mm. These obligations are not debt principal, but they reduce downside flexibility if revenue growth or NRM disappoints. 15. Forward model summary

Management long-term view

The 2023 Investor Day framework targeted $1.4bn of FY2026 adjusted EBITDA and $2.1bn in FY2028 from existing states, while the live FY2026 guide is $700mm-$900mm after $200mm-$300mm of Predictions investment. The 2026 Investor Day framework described a longer-term 30%+ adjusted EBITDA margin model with cost of revenue, sales and marketing, and expense leverage. [expectations-internal.md; deck-analyst.md]

[expectations-internal.md; assumption-refuter.md]

16. Risks

Sports outcome, hold, and NRM risk

Sports outcome and monetization volatility are immediate operating risks. Q2 2025 sportsbook-friendly outcomes added roughly $100mm of revenue, while September and October 2025 customer-friendly outcomes reduced revenue by more than $300mm. Q4 2024 NFL outcomes created a roughly $250mm revenue and $175mm adjusted EBITDA headwind. The risk is not variance alone; promotions, handle, NRM, and customer behavior interact. [risks.md; phase3_adversarial_summary.json]

The leading indicators are Sportsbook handle growth, NRM, gross hold where disclosed, parlay mix, live-betting mix, promo reinvestment, and handle per MUP. A bad signal is NRM improvement paired with flat or negative handle growth, because that can mean monetization is being bought with lower customer engagement. [risks.md; business-model--sportsbook.md]

Predictions and regulatory risk

Prediction Markets are a major new regulatory and execution risk. DraftKings operates under CFTC/NFA oversight, owns Railbird as a DCM foundation, and has warned that litigation or regulatory action could require it to stop offering specific event contracts. Management is investing $200mm-$300mm in FY2026 before reporting steady-state economics. [risks.md; phase3_adversarial_summary.json]

Favorable federal treatment can expand reach beyond traditional sportsbook states and reduce tax leakage; adverse state, tribal, league, or court outcomes can leave DraftKings with spending, technology, and compliance cost without a broad product. The leading indicators are allowed contract scope, customer overlap, volume by state type, take rate, market-maker share, revenue, and losses or collateral tied to market making. [technology.md; business-model--other.md]

Tax, platform, and market-access risk

Taxes and market access are structural. FY2025 Sportsbook and iGaming were 93% of revenue, and Q1 2026 gaming taxes alone were $569.5mm. State tax increases, skin costs, market-access requirements, federal excise tax, and platform availability can all change contribution profit. The 2024 surcharge reversal showed that customer and competitor response can block simple tax pass-through. [risks.md; value-chain.md; phase3_adversarial_summary.json]

Platform and vendor risk is less visible but operationally real. Cloud service, app-store distribution, payment processing, chargebacks, sports data, supplier games, and compliance tools can affect uptime, bet acceptance, user trust, and regulatory standing. The leading indicators are outages, payment failures, app-store policy changes, supplier contract changes, and state-level gross-margin movement. [technology.md; risks.md]

iGaming execution risk

iGaming execution risk matters because casino is strategically important and under-disclosed. DraftKings has proprietary studio content, embedded distribution, and a large wallet, but Q1 2026 growth slowed to 8.9%, management acknowledged market lag, and state-level share is missing. [competitive-advantage--igaming.md; business-model--igaming.md]

The risk is that DraftKings spends more on iCasino-first acquisition and supplier content without restoring market-relative growth or improving gross margin. Leading indicators are iGaming revenue growth versus AGA and FanDuel/Caesars, embedded-handle mix, in-house studio handle mix, supplier cost, casino-first customer acquisition, and state-level share. [competitive-landscape--igaming.md; assumption-refuter.md]

Responsible-gaming, litigation, and consumer protection

Responsible-gaming, VIP, promotional, privacy, cybersecurity, payment, and consumer-protection risks remain active. The risk workpaper identifies litigation and regulatory actions in several states and municipalities, including allegations around vulnerable-user targeting and promotional practices. These issues can raise compliance cost, restrict promotions, change VIP economics, and damage brand trust. [risks.md; technology.md]

The leading indicators are complaint volume, settlement cost, changes in state marketing rules, limits on promotions, VIP restrictions, chargeback rates, and any requirement to change user onboarding or affordability checks. The owner should watch these alongside Sportsbook NRM because higher monetization that triggers regulatory backlash is not durable. [risks.md]

Capital allocation and dilution risk

Capital allocation risk is rising with cash generation. Acquisition returns are not separately measurable, repurchases have not yet reduced diluted shares, and the 2028 convertible refinancing may add cash interest. The risk case is sustained SBC, acquisitions or new products that consume cash, higher debt cost, and limited net share reduction. [capital-allocation.md; capitalization.md]

The leading indicators are diluted shares, Class A shares outstanding, SBC as a percentage of revenue and adjusted EBITDA, repurchase price and share count, FCF after capitalized software per share, acquisition consideration, contingent consideration, and convert refinancing terms. [capitalization.md; assumption-refuter.md]

Correlated bad-quarter scenario

A realistic bad quarter combines several risks at once: customer-friendly sports outcomes lower NRM, high-tax states pressure promotions, iGaming growth remains below market, Predictions spend ramps before revenue disclosure, and share-based compensation keeps diluted shares from declining. In that scenario, revenue can still grow while adjusted EBITDA, FCF after capitalized software, and FCF per share disappoint. [risks.md; modeler.md; assumption-refuter.md]
