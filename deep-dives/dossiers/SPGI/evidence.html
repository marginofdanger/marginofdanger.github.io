Evidence

Claims by section
business-model
  • The market-geared share did most of the recent work: of the $2,839M revenue increase from 2023 to 2025, 53% came from the ~31% of revenue that is issuance-, AUM-, or volume-linked (10-K FY2025 revenue-type table).
  • The revenue equation is: transaction revenue = billed issuance x realized fee rate, plus a non-transaction annuity (surveillance, annual fees, Ratings Evaluation Services, majority-owned Crisil, and the $172M royalty MI pays to resell Ratings content). FY2025 split: transaction $2,470M (52%), non-transaction $2,254M (48%) (10-K FY2025, Ratings MD&A).
  • S&P's published US fee schedule lists 8.35 bps with a $155,000 minimum on most US corporate transactions as of January 2026, up 3.1% from 8.10 bps a year earlier.
  • Realized incremental margins ran 68-105% in the up-years from 2019 (2017, an up-year distorted by legal-cost noise, ran 56%), with a symmetric 91% decremental in 2022 — the down-year removed $1,047M of revenue and $957M of operating profit, and margin declined 9.4pp in one year (10-K segment tables, FY2016-FY2025).
  • MI sells subscription data and workflow across three lines — Data, Analytics & Insights (Capital IQ Pro desktop, feeds/APIs, Visible Alpha, With Intelligence); Enterprise Solutions (loan-market and private-markets workflow: Wall Street Office, ClearPar, iLevel); and Credit & Risk Solutions (resale of Ratings content via RatingsXpress/RatingsDirect) — with FY2025 revenue and segment share below (quarterly-update decks, sums tie to the 10-K segment total).
  • Pricing is enterprise value-based, not per-seat: "we don't do the headcount model... we want a fair share of that value" (Investor Day 2025-11-13, MI president Saha).
  • Disclosed price evidence: organic ACV growth ~6% for six straight quarters, net renewal rates up ~100bp y/y (level undisclosed), two clients paid 35-45% renewal increases for AI-ready data access, and one buy-side client displaced an incumbent at a ~20% premium (Q1 2026 call).
  • S&P Dow Jones Indices licenses index IP three ways (FY2025) — asset-linked fees (bp-of-AUM fees from ETF and mutual-fund sponsors), exchange-traded-derivatives royalties (CME pays a profit share on its equity-index complex, $193M in 2025; the ~$131M residual is mostly Cboe SPX options, terms undisclosed), and data subscriptions — with FY2025 revenue and segment share below.
  • The blended proxy rate (asset-linked fees ÷ average ETF AUM) declined from ~4.4bp (2016) and 3.85bp (2020) to 2.53bp (2025) and 2.43bp annualized in Q1 2026 — roughly −6%/yr for nine consecutive years.
  • The ETD royalty is the volatility hedge: $125M (2016) → $324M (2025), +18% in Q1 2026 on record SPX volumes, and +38% in 2022 when equities declined 19% — the segment grew revenue 17% in the worst equity year of the decade.
  • Every marginal profit dollar loses ~25% to the CME/CGIS interest: FY2025 operating profit $1,271M less $322M NCI leaves $949M, a 51.3% net margin.
  • GAAP margin 41.0% FY2025 (adjusted ≈ 47.5%), up from 35% in 2022 on 55% realized incremental adjusted margins.
  • The data estate (38B records from 177,000 sources, ~6M records added daily) is acquired substantially by barter — service shops and police agencies contribute records in exchange for products and brand exposure — so marginal report cost is ~zero and the biggest cash cost is brand and selling ($1.5B cumulative media spend since 2000).
  • Standalone economics (Form-10 basis via the 2026-05-12 Investor Day): 40.6% adjusted EBITDA margin, flat for three years by choice ("growth comes first"); FCF conversion only 63-65% of adjusted EBITDA because the IHS merger left a $1.0B deferred tax liability costing $80-90M/yr of cash for ~12 years.
competitive-advantage
  • Ratings and Indices are structural advantages of the first class — the reference class of franchises durable against well-funded attackers that support pricing power and throw off excess returns with almost no defensive reinvestment — each with specific, quantified qualifications.
  • Most investment guidelines and index-inclusion rules name Fitch, Moody's and S&P specifically — DBRS states that "with limited exception, all major indices exclude securities that do not have a credit rating from Fitch, Moody's, or S&P" (SEC OCR 2025 Staff Report, pp. 29-30, archived) — so an issuer of index-eligible public debt cannot substitute away at any price a challenger could offer.
  • The advantage passed the hardest available test: the structured-finance ratings failure of the financial crisis cost $1.6B in legal and regulatory settlements (charged 2014-2015) and did not dislodge share or pricing — S&P still held 49.6% of all NRSRO ratings outstanding at end-2024 and raised realized prices throughout.
  • In structured finance, where one or two ratings suffice and five agencies compete deal-by-deal, S&P's US CLO share declined from 60.5% to 47.0% and CMBS from 38.1% to 13.0% over 2023-2025 (SEC OCR 2025 report, Green Street data); in private credit the index-inclusion mechanism does not operate, rating shopping is "more acute" per the SEC, and the volume leader in middle-market assessments is KBRA.
  • S&P holds no material economic edge over Moody's — adjusted margins 65% vs 63.6% in FY2025, a gap that narrowed from ~2.9pp in 2024; revenue per analyst only ~12% higher; the margin gap oscillating within ±3pp of parity across 2021-2025.
  • The financials are what that structure predicts: a 63.8% GAAP segment margin (FY2025), ~80% incremental margins across the decade, price capture disclosed in nine of ten years, and $4,724M of revenue produced on $1,137M of segment assets and $64M of capex.
  • The core asset is not index calculation (a commodity skill — CRSP proved an academic shop can supply Vanguard) but the S&P 500 liquidity ecosystem: $5.48T of linked ETF AUM at end-2025, an exclusive ownership-linked CME futures/options license that is effectively perpetual while CME holds its JV stake, and a benchmark name that is itself the product — an S&P 500 fund that switches index ceases to be an S&P 500 fund.
  • First, the advantage produces quantity, not unit price: the realized asset-linked rate has declined roughly 6-8%/yr for nine years (proxy 4.07bp in 2019 -> 2.53bp in 2025 -> 2.43bp annualized Q1 2026) because concentrated buyers negotiate hard and flows concentrate in the cheapest tier.
  • Thirteen years after Vanguard demonstrated the switching move against MSCI, no flagship S&P 500 product has defected, and the segment converts the position into a 68.7% GAAP margin with essentially no capital.
  • a 10-12% medium-term growth target that embeds 5-7% annual market appreciation
  • MI's organic growth has been pinned at ~6% for three years and Q1 2026 - the same rate as measured global financial-market-data spend (+6.5% in 2025, Burton-Taylor [web]) - so MI holds share rather than compounding it.
  • The adjusted margin was 34.1% in 2021 and 34.0% in 2025: zero net expansion across four post-merger years, with no premium over Moody's Analytics (33.1% adjusted FY2025, expanding faster at +240bp) or FactSet (32.2% GAAP FY2025), while MSCI operates ~20 points higher.
  • Three niches hold genuine advantage: mission-critical loan and private-markets workflow (WSO, ClearPar, iLevel - industry plumbing with multi-party network effects, +14% organic in Q1 2026); exclusive commercial distribution of S&P Ratings content (rides the upstream duopoly; +6%); and unreproducible historical archives (Compustat, SNL, point-in-time fundamentals).
  • CARFAX: a contributory database that cannot be backfilled (history is the barrier), 36 OEM certified-pre-owned programs that will not certify a car without a CARFAX report, consumer pull of 2M "show me the CARFAX" requests a month, and realized consumer pricing of $44.99 per report against Experian AutoCheck's $29.99 - a ~1.5x premium sustained against the only substantive rival
  • both franchises are nearly capital-free at the operating level (capex 0.5%/1.6% of revenue) and both expanded margins every year since 2022 — but SPGI's balance sheet holds $8,543M of segment assets against Energy and $12,974M against Mobility, mostly merger goodwill, so returns on the capital actually paid run roughly 8.5% for Energy and 2.3-4.1% for Mobility [estimate: segment NOPAT ÷ segment assets at the 22.6% consolidated tax rate] against an 8-9% hurdle.
competitive-landscape
  • The industry is a big-three oligopoly at the core and a five-player market in non-government categories.
  • Who sets price: the agencies list-price and issuers pay; the realized take-rate went 5.3bp (2022) -> 5.7-5.8bp (2025/Q1 2026) with "improved contract terms" disclosed in 2024, 2025 and Q1 2026.
  • Global index-industry revenue was a record $7.2B in 2025 (+13.4%), with the top three holding over two-thirds: S&P DJI 25.7%, MSCI 24.9%, FTSE Russell 17.9%, Nasdaq 11.5%; HHI ~= 1,750-1,850 [estimate] (Burton-Taylor via trade press [web]).
  • S&P DJI-linked AUM grew 25% in 2025 against +33.7% for the global ETF industry ($19.85T, ETFGI [web]) - a share-of-flows loss as 2025-26 flows rotated to international/EM (MSCI's home ground) and active ETFs (which pay index providers little) reached a record $1.86T.
  • The measured market reached $49.2B in 2025 (+6.5%), with Bloomberg (~30% share, ~$13.3B 2024 revenue [web, press estimates]) and LSEG Data & Analytics together holding about half (Burton-Taylor via trade press [web]).
  • The per-seat gap is the cleanest expression of MI's desktop position: Bloomberg monetizes ~$37K per terminal per year; SPGI's entire MI segment divided by its 350,000 Cap IQ Pro users is ~$14K - and that numerator includes workflow and ratings-resale revenue unrelated to the desktop [estimate: division of disclosed totals].
  • the first credible collective attack on the loan-workflow niche, mounted by the agent banks that feed it data.
  • Platts is the clear #1 PRA — 15,000+ daily assessments, 250+ IOSCO benchmarks, the management-claimed ~70% of waterborne crude — with Argus Media the #2 and fastest gap-closer (UK-entity turnover +27.9% in FY Jun-2024 [web, weak source]; the Aramco ASCI win its flagship precedent).
  • the one pool where SPGI trails — Enverus (>$500M ARR, relationships with 98% of US producers [web]) out-scales S&P's ~$415-440M line, and SPGI chose divestiture-plus-repositioning (software to SLB, CERA Titan) over contesting workflow software.
  • a two-player market in which CARFAX (38B records, 40,000 dealers) dominates Experian AutoCheck (~11B+ records [web]; that count could not be re-verified — Experian's page no longer shows it); the legacy Polk registration business provides core services to >90% of US market volume (management claim).
  • management's own scorecard concedes it — the 2022 target of 7-9% growth in a 5-6% market delivered ~6%, and the 2025 reset to 6-8% re-asserts share-taking with a zero-for-one record.
expectations
  • The headline disagreement: consensus prices the very top of management's guidance ($19.63 CY2026 EPS vs a $19.40-19.65 guide) and then accelerates above it (+13.2% CY2027, +12.0% CY2028) - a path that requires the issuance cycle not to bite after the 2026 record-compare year, while management itself pre-announces billed-issuance growth turning negative in Q4 2026.
financial-history
  • MI's ACV series (trough ~4% in Q4 2024, recovery to 6.5-7%, tick down to ~6% in Q1 2026) is the single series that will settle the AI-substitution question.
m-and-a
  • The buying record has one defining bet, the $43.5B all-stock IHS Markit merger, which over-delivered on synergies and is being partly reversed on portfolio: disclosed exits equal ~46% of IHSM's FY2021 revenue and likely more than half including the undisclosed pieces, with the record's only segment-scale realized loss (Engineering Solutions, -$120M pre-tax vs merger-date fair value) on an acquired unit.
risks
  • The 10-K's AI risk language escalated three consecutive years and roughly tripled in FY2025 — evidence management does not treat it as noise, whatever the calls say.
secular-trends
  • The through-cycle decomposition strips the cycle out: Ratings transaction revenue grew +2.3%/yr peak-to-peak (2021→2025) and −2.1%/yr trough-to-trough (2018→2022); the durable engine is the non-transaction annuity (+5.5%/yr over 2017-2025, worst years −0.3% and −1.9%) plus disclosed pricing.
  • The old framing — private credit disintermediates rated public debt — is now partly a monetized vector: enterprise private-markets revenue exceeded $600M in FY2025 and Ratings private-credit revenue grew 25% in Q1 2026, against a market Preqin projects from $2.28T to ~$4.5T by 2030 [web].
  • hyperscaler capex debt lifted IG billed issuance 41% in Q1 2026 while AI-disruption fear cut tech-adjacent leveraged loans — AI is currently a net Ratings demand tailwind with a mix cost.
  • The 2026 Energy guide was nonetheless cut to 4.5-6% on the Iran conflict and sanctions — the second consecutive year of downward in-year Energy revisions — while the volatility-geared sleeves (GTS ~+30%, royalties +27%, record CERAWeek) did the quarter's work.
technology
  • Management quantified the mix at the 2025 Investor Day: over 95% of revenue from proprietary benchmarks, data and tools; benchmarks are the majority of revenue and over 70% of operating income
  • The same analysis at the MI level is less flattering: 12% of MI revenue is undifferentiated (~$590M [estimate: 12% x $4,902M]) and another 23% is redistributed Ratings content (Q1 2026 deck, slide 11)
  • The desktop is the most substitutable layer in the company: management sizes Cap IQ Pro at about 6% of enterprise revenue (Q4 2025 call), roughly $920M.
  • the customer-facing LLM-ready API reached 300+ customers under contract or trial within ~3 quarters of launch, with API call volume up 5x quarter-over-quarter (Q1 2026 call).
  • S&P was a launch partner for Claude for Financial Services (July 2025) and now distributes licensed data into Claude, Microsoft Copilot, Google Gemini Enterprise, and OpenAI via MCP connectors, with no-training clauses and license gating ("we don't allow the LLM providers to train on S&P Global data" - Q4 2025 call).
  • 15,000+ daily assessments from 450+ reporters; ~70% of global waterborne crude trade is priced using Platts benchmarks (management claim, Investor Day 2025-11-13).
  • they are currently the fastest-growing part of MI - Enterprise Solutions grew 14% organically in Q1 2026 - which cuts against the thesis that workflow software is the most AI-vulnerable layer.
value-chain
  • SPGI sits at the benchmark/IP layer of four chains, and the margin ladder across its own positions summarizes the profit-pool split: the benchmark layer earns 64-69% operating margins (Indices consolidated 68.7%, Ratings 63.8%), the vertical-data layer earns 41% GAAP (Energy), and the aggregation layer earns ~20% GAAP / 34% adjusted (MI) — and the aggregation layer is the only one whose suppliers are gaining pricing power.
  • An end investor in SPY pays a 9.45bp expense ratio; 3.0bp plus $600K/yr goes to S&P DJI — roughly a third of everything the investor pays (SPY filings, archived from EDGAR).
  • On the derivatives leg, CME pays the JV a profit share ($193M in 2025, ≈$0.18 per S&P/Dow-complex contract [estimate]) and receives back $322M as 27% owner — the licensee is a net collector of ~$129M before its own trading economics.
  • Customer ROI is overwhelming: a $1,000M 10-year IG issue pays ~$571K once at the realized 5.7bp rate — about 0.6bp/yr of par, ~1% of one year's interest cost [estimate: realized rate applied to a representative deal] — and two big-agency ratings are effectively market-access infrastructure for index-eligible debt.
  • Upstream payments identified in filings: AWS ($1.0B five-year commitment, ~$200M/yr — operational concentration rather than financial lock-in), third-party data suppliers (no dollars disclosed, but the 10-K states supplier consolidation "has... resulted in an increase in fees charged" and that many suppliers are also competitors), and the $172M internal Ratings royalty.
