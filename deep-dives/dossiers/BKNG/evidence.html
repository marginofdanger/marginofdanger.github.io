Evidence

Claims by section
business-model
  • Revenue is recognized net — commission and margin only — at the time of travel (check-in), not at booking.
  • Merchant revenue per booking is economically higher than agency, not lower; the 13.7% merchant ratio looks low only because flights (a merchant vertical with a low take) sit in that denominator
  • Merchant gross bookings grew 24.8% in FY2025 while agency gross bookings declined 8.7% — the multi-year agency-to-merchant migration continues.
  • Supply is not the constraint — Booking.com listed ~4.4M properties at year-end (~500k hotels plus ~3.9M alternative-accommodation homes), up from ~4.0M — so adding inventory is cheap and fast.
  • The take rate declined ~150 basis points from 15.7% in 2018 to a 14.1% trough in 2022, then clawed back ~40 basis points to 14.5% by 2025 as payments monetization edged out flight dilution.
  • The Connected-Trip push into flights is structurally take-rate-dilutive, which is why the blended take sits ~110 basis points below the pre-pandemic 15.6% even as payments revenue lifts it back.
  • Marketing is the largest and most variable cost — $8,186M, 30.4% of revenue, 4.4% of gross bookings — and management calls it "substantially variable"
  • realized incremental operating margins ran 40% over 2024→2025 and ~38% over 2022→2025, above the ~33% average margin but not dramatically so.
  • The business runs on a negative-working-capital float: under the merchant model the traveler pays at booking while BKNG pays the supplier at or after check-in, so deferred merchant bookings ($5,270M at year-end 2025, up from $4,031M) plus accounts payable exceed operating current assets.
competitive-advantage
  • Booking Holdings has a real, wide, and demonstrably durable competitive advantage, but it sits on the strong end of contestable rather than in the structural, un-attackable tier of Visa, Mastercard, or Google search.
  • ~33% GAAP operating margin and ~34% FCF margin, sustained for fifteen-plus years across COVID and the mobile and metasearch transitions, run on negative invested capital, with 1.2% capex intensity throwing off ~$9B of annual free cash flow.
  • That is 2.6x Expedia's operating margin (12.7%) and above Airbnb's (20.8%), and it is being defended while BKNG gains share on its largest Western peer (FY2025 revenue +13.4% and room nights +8% versus Expedia +7.6% and room nights +7%).
  • Roughly 30% of revenue is a marketing tax paid largely to a single more-powerful frenemy, Google.
  • Operating margin is ~7 points below its 2014 level of ~40% and a few points below its 2018 peak of ~37% even adjusted
  • The binding constraint is demand-side traffic economics, and BKNG is winning it right now — rising direct mix, falling marketing intensity — which is why the evidence supports "wide moat, currently defended successfully," not "eroding."
  • the asset-light OTAs run on negative invested capital: BKNG's operating working capital is roughly −$9.8B (deferred merchant bookings and payables exceed receivables), and adding PP&E, leases, goodwill, and intangibles still leaves operating invested capital negative at ~−$4.7B.
  • The advantages with the clearest evidence of widening are marketing efficiency (improving) and the absolute scale gap versus Expedia and Tripadvisor (wide, stable).
competitive-landscape
  • Booking Holdings is the scale leader of global online accommodation distribution and, over FY2022-25, is simultaneously the largest and the fastest-growing of the three Western OTA majors: FY2025 gross bookings of $186.1B grew a three-year CAGR of 15.4%, ahead of Airbnb's gross booking value at +13.0% and roughly double Expedia's +8.0%.
  • It leads in absolute room-night volume (1,235M, ~2.3x Airbnb's 533M nights) and earns the highest take rate of the three (14.5% versus Airbnb 13.4% and Expedia 12.3%).
  • The single most important competitive fact is not a rival OTA at all — it is Google (and emerging AI assistants), the demand-side gatekeeper that intermediates a large share of traffic and captures ~30% of BKNG revenue as marketing spend.
  • Western accommodations is an effective duopoly of Booking.com and Expedia in hotels plus Airbnb dominating alternative accommodations; Asia-Pacific is led by Trip.com in China (where foreign OTAs are largely excluded by regulation), with Agoda and Trip.com contesting the rest of the region and MakeMyTrip dominant in India.
  • Management's ">$3 trillion TAM" framing (from the April 2026 investor deck) is marketing: it includes ~$900B of food & beverage and total consumer travel spend rather than bookable gross bookings.
  • in Q1 2026 Airbnb nights grew +9% versus Booking.com and Expedia room nights +6% each — Airbnb is now taking incremental volume share at ~1.5x BKNG's growth.
  • Booking.com's ~445M alt-accom room nights [estimate: 36% mix × total] are now in the same order of magnitude as Airbnb's 533M total nights, but Airbnb owns the category brand and grows faster (Q1 2026 nights +9% versus Booking.com alt-accom +5.5%), and this segment structurally lowers BKNG's margins.
  • OpenTable is the US leader but its share slipped from ~51% (2022) to ~40-46% as Amex (Resy + Tock) and DoorDash (SevenRooms) bundle reservations into payments and delivery ecosystems.
critical-points
  • The 15% adjusted-EPS ambition is ~55% operating and ~45% financial engineering.
expectations
  • Across the thirteen calls from Q1 2023 to Q1 2026, adjusted EBITDA beat the high end of its own next-quarter guidance in every single quarter (12 of 12 above the high end, often by 5-28%), with zero outright misses on any headline metric; room nights beat the high end in 10 of 12 quarters, revenue in 9 of 9.
risks
  • BKNG's binding constraint is customer acquisition, and traffic flows through third parties (chiefly Google) that are also its biggest competitor, via three escalating vectors: zero-click answers that satisfy travel intent in-surface, self-preferencing of platforms' own travel offerings, and agentic disintermediation.
  • Google already disintermediated metasearch (Tripadvisor cut its outlook, Trivago filed an antitrust suit, Google's US metasearch share rose ~51%→~62%), and BKNG's own KAYAK is a victim of the same force.
secular-trends
  • The single most important secular fact is that underlying volume growth has structurally declined. Room nights compounded ~15%/yr pre-COVID (2016-2019) but only ~6.5%/yr peak-to-peak (2019→2025), and +8% in FY2025.
  • Management's own long-term ambition — 8% constant-currency gross bookings, 8% revenue, 15% adjusted EPS — codifies the step-down: it is roughly half the pre-COVID gross-bookings run-rate
  • the alternative-accommodation mix shift (~30% in 2022 to ~38% of Booking.com room nights in Q1 2026)
  • The merchant/payments mix rose from 27% of gross bookings (2019) to 72% (Q1 2026), a durable positive re-rating of the take rate (payment rebates in merchant revenue) that builds the deferred-booking float. But the shift to merchant is a one-time gain: it stops adding to growth at the ~78% ceiling
  • AI assistants "may reduce consumers choosing to visit dedicated online travel platforms… reducing direct traffic, bookings, and customer relationships… AI agents may further evolve into full-service booking platforms… disintermediating OTCs"
technology
  • Booking Holdings does not run on proprietary technology the way a chip designer does.
  • What differentiates the company is scale, brand, supply breadth, a direct-traffic distribution base, and a decade-plus store of proprietary transaction data, executed through solid at-scale engineering. This is a distribution-and-marketing business with applied technology, not a technology business with distribution.
  • Its difficulty is threefold: connectivity to ~4.4M mostly unsophisticated properties kept live in real time, payment-rail breadth with fraud and chargeback management, and regulatory licensing across dozens of jurisdictions (EU PSD3, DMA gatekeeper status).
  • connected transactions (spanning more than one vertical) grew high-teens percent in Q1 2026, roughly 3x Booking.com's total transaction growth, but remain a "low double-digit percentage" of Booking.com transactions
  • the 10-K states plainly that "competing offerings can be launched at a relatively low cost, with the pace of innovation increasing and the cost to do so decreasing as a result of Gen AI"
  • The only P&L-visible benefit so far is cost, not revenue: customer-service cost per booking down ~10% company-wide and a double-digit reduction at Agoda from AI automation
  • OpenAI launched agentic "Instant Checkout" in February 2026 and retreated from completing transactions within weeks, concluding that travel commerce is too complex, fragmented, and payment-heavy for an agent to intermediate directly
  • Level 2/3 Genius members were over 30% of the active base and a "high 50%" share of room nights over the trailing four quarters, up year over year, and these tiers "book direct at a meaningfully higher rate"
value-chain
  • It captures ~14.5 cents of every travel dollar booked through its OTC brands, converts about a third of that take to operating profit (~4.7 cents of the travel dollar), and holds almost no inventory.
  • The new leading indicator, disclosed in the Q1 2026 10-Q, is that organic/unpaid search (SEO) traffic is now declining
  • the DMA parity ban (EEA) and the 2024 Spanish CNMC restriction end that protection and let hotels undercut BKNG's channel. This is the clearest structural erosion at the supplier interface.
