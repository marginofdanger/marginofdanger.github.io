Evidence

Claims by section
business-model
  • Roblox operates a single platform on which users play and socialize in experiences made largely by third-party creators.
  • Users acquire Robux and spend it on virtual items and access; creators receive earned Robux and can convert qualifying balances into cash.
  • Advertising, licensing, and brand activity existed in FY2025, but Roblox said those bookings were insignificant.
  • This identity is more useful than GAAP revenue for current demand because durable-item sales enter bookings immediately and revenue over time.
  • FY2025 had 127.0 million average DAUs, 29.025 million average monthly payers, 22.85% implied monthly conversion, and $19.49 of formula-compatible monthly bookings per payer, producing $6.788 billion of bookings.
  • That is material because 82% of FY2025 DAUs were outside the United States and Canada while only 39% of revenue was international.
  • Users materially fund the business before GAAP recognition. Deferred revenue of $6.506 billion less deferred payment costs of $1.281 billion produced $5.225 billion of net deferred funding at FY2025; the balance reached $5.492 billion at Q1 2026.
competitive-advantage
  • Roblox has a defensible advantage today, but it remains open to erosion.
  • The audience and social graph attract creators; creator output expands content; discovery and the economy connect supply with demand; and the integrated stack reduces the work required to publish across devices. That combination is harder to replace than any individual engine, language, or title.
  • The evidence does not support a structural position comparable with Visa, Google Search, or Meta's mature networks because users and creators can multi-home, Epic has an integrated substitute, Apple and Google control device distribution and payment channels, and Roblox has not produced positive GAAP operating profit or interpretable ROIC.
  • The financial record supports scale economics more strongly than operating returns.
  • Gross margin rose from 74.0% in FY2020 to 78.1% in FY2025, and the revenue-based platform contribution margin rose from 9.9% to 23.8%. Operating margin improved only 3.6 points over the same period to negative 25.2%.
  • The advantage should persist while creators earn more expected value on Roblox than elsewhere, users retain meaningful social connections, discovery improves without sacrificing trust, and distribution fees remain contained.
competitive-landscape
  • Roblox competes in two overlapping markets: user attention and creator economics. Both permit multi-homing.
  • Using bookings as a spend proxy, Roblox's share increased from about 2.36% in 2024 to 3.37% in 2025, about 101 basis points.
  • Roblox led the disclosed gaming-UGC set on annual hours and creator payouts, but the periods are not synchronized.
  • Roblox reported 23,500 creators receiving fiat payments, while its Creator Hub said the top 1,000 averaged $1.3 million and the median DevEx creator received $1,550 in 2025.
  • Fortnite/UEFN is the strongest disclosed competitor for creator supply. Unity competes more directly in tools; Apple and Google set important transaction terms; YouTube and Meta compete for attention and creator time. Roblox does not control the device-distribution channel, and no creator contract requires exclusivity.
critical-points
  • The central operating question is whether the age-check and discovery effects are temporary or whether some 2025 user and spending growth came at the expense of later periods.
  • From FY2023 through FY2025, DAUs contributed an estimated 93.9% of modeled bookings growth and daily-payer conversion contributed 19.6%, while daily bookings per payer subtracted 13.4%. Q1 2026 bookings per monthly payer declined 5.7% y/y to $18.78.
  • By FY2032 the same base reaches 39.0% FCF/bookings while lowering payment, creator, infrastructure, R&D, G&A, SBC, capex, and dilution ratios together. The joint path is not supported by the historical record or the outside-view 18%-22% FY2028 FCF/bookings range.
expectations
  • The larger expectation is the reacceleration to about 19%-20% bookings growth in FY2027-FY2028 and FCF/bookings above 21.9% by FY2028. The driver-based base model is 2.5%-3.3% below the FY2027 public bookings range and 5.0%-6.0% below FY2028.
  • Across Q2 2024-Q1 2026, revenue beat the available internal estimate in all eight quarters, FCF beat in all eight, bookings beat seven and met one, and adjusted EBITDA beat six, met one, and missed one. FY2025 actual bookings exceeded the initial guidance high end by $1.488 billion, while actual Adjusted EBITDA was $65 million below the initial guidance low end. Demand upside and cost conversion therefore require separate assessment.
financial-history
  • Revenue increased 9.6x from FY2019 to FY2025, but operating profit stayed negative. FCF moved from $14 million to $1.353 billion, supported by upfront collections and SBC.
  • Q3-Q4 2025 show the viral peak: bookings growth reached 70% and 63%, DAU growth about 70%, and payer counts increased sharply. Q1 2026 retained 43% bookings growth without a comparable new viral title, but sequential DAUs, hours, and payers declined.
forward-model
  • The model's five principal drivers are average DAU growth, monthly payer conversion, monthly bookings per payer, DevEx as a percentage of bookings, and infrastructure cost per engaged hour.
  • The FY2026 base is the part of the model with the strongest support: $7.460 billion of bookings, $6.002 billion of revenue, $1.018 billion operating loss, and $1.163 billion of FCF. Bookings and revenue sit near current guidance, and FCF is within management's $1.050-$1.275 billion range and the internal $1.150-$1.300 billion range.
  • The negative $300 million base other-cash adjustment is selected to calibrate FY2026 FCF after deferred funding, receivables, payables, and capex are separately modeled. It is not management guidance for an operating line and has no disclosed forward bridge. Leases, creator liabilities, legal cash timing, tax, compensation, and other working-capital items should be modeled individually before the residual is relied on. It is the largest unresolved FY2026 cash limitation.
  • Treating both terminal base and bull cases as central outcomes is not supported. Base terminal bookings growth is about 12%, close to reference-class medians. The unsupported part is the simultaneous improvement in conversion, spend, payment cost, DevEx, infrastructure unit cost, R&D intensity, G&A intensity, SBC intensity, capex intensity, dilution, and the cash residual. The outside-view FY2028 FCF/bookings range is 18%-22%.
  • The historical best annual GAAP incremental operating margin was 24.4% in FY2024. Base FY2026 is 19.3%, but every base year from FY2027 through FY2032 is between 36.2% and 44.8%. The base case's incremental-margin path is weakly supported.
  • Adding one month to paying-user life reduces FY2032 operating income by about $75 million but FCF by only $3 million. That sensitivity illustrates the model's accounting/cash separation.
management-claim-pressure-tests
  • In Q1 2026, management separated healthy monetization and retention from weaker new-user acquisition, communication, and discovery. The undated ambitions lack denominator bridges, and the dilution goals have not reconciled to reported share counts.
management-incentives-capital-allocation
  • The beneficial-ownership table gave Baszucki all 47,070,464 Class B shares and 58.8% of total voting power as of February 13, 2026.
  • The regular executive program has no annual cash incentive. Baszucki's salary was $0 in 2025, and 100% of his direct compensation was equity. His annual award was intended to be 75% performance stock units and 25% restricted stock units.
  • The plan rewarded growth and non-GAAP profitability. It did not measure per-share results, share issuance, SBC, GAAP income, or relative shareholder return. The maximum payout therefore coincided with FY2025 SBC of $1.129 billion and 6.3% year-end share growth.
  • Roblox reinvested rather than returning cash. FY2019-FY2025 OCF totaled $4.729 billion, PP&E capex totaled $1.648 billion, and acquisition/intangible cash was $149 million. Neither discretionary repurchases nor dividends offset dilution.
  • Per-share operating progress has existed because growth outran issuance, not because issuance was controlled.
secular-trends
  • The pre-FY2025 baseline is FY2022-FY2024: bookings CAGR was 23.3% and DAU CAGR about 21.7%. FY2025 then increased bookings 55%, DAUs 53%, and hours 69%.
  • The FY2025 acceleration was not confined to the two largest viral experiences. In Q1 2026, experiences outside the top ten produced 43% engagement growth, 41% spending growth, and 65% of spending growth, without a comparable new viral hit.
  • Those figures show breadth, while the top-50 share of hours rising to 51% in December 2025 shows increasing concentration.
  • International reach is durable, but the geographic mix lowers revenue per DAU. Outside the United States and Canada, users were 82% of FY2025 DAUs and 39% of revenue; Q1 2026 was 83% and 42%. Revenue per DAU outside the domestic region was roughly 14%-16% of the domestic level.
  • Age expansion is harder to measure after the methodology change. Historical age data used self-declared information; the current series uses checked age. The break coincided with communication restrictions and prevents older-user growth from being compared on a consistent basis.
  • Advertising, commerce, and licensing are not included as modeled growth drivers because FY2025 advertising and licensing bookings remained insignificant in disclosed results.
technology
  • Roblox combines creation tools, hosting, distribution, discovery, monetization, and moderation across devices in one system.
  • Individual components have alternatives or are open source; the differentiation comes from integrating them.
  • Q1 2026 infrastructure and trust-and-safety expense was $324 million on 31.0 billion hours, or about 1.05 cents per hour, versus about 1.12 cents a year earlier, a 6.4% improvement.
value-chain
  • Roblox sets the Robux purchase price, in-experience allocation rules, and DevEx cash-out rate. Creators set the content and virtual-item offer. Apple, Google, and other payment channels deduct fees from affected purchases before Roblox receives the proceeds. Users pay before much of the accounting revenue appears.
  • Q1 2026 DevEx already reached 24.4% of bookings and increased 50%, faster than bookings growth of 43%.
  • Management identified user acquisition as the binding near-term constraint in Q1 2026: engagement, monetization, and retention remained strong, while age-check communication friction and monetization-biased discovery weakened new sign-ups.
  • Creator economics are the more visible long-run margin constraint because rival platforms can recruit supply with higher payouts.
  • User prepayments increased net deferred funding to $5.225 billion at FY2025 and $5.492 billion at Q1 2026. This favorable working capital creates a persistent gap between bookings, GAAP revenue, operating income, and company-defined free cash flow.
