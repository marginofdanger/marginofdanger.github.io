Claims by section
business-model
  • CoStar information and analytics was $1,259 million (38.8%), LoopNet $312 million (9.6%), Other Commercial Real Estate $216 million (6.7%), and Residential Real Estate $1,460 million (45.0%)
  • The Commercial reportable segment was $1,787 million of revenue earning $672 million of Adjusted EBITDA, a 37.6% margin; Residential was nearly as large in revenue but lost $230 million on an Adjusted EBITDA basis
  • Commercial customers — brokers, owners, tenants, appraisers, lenders, investors, REIT managers, hotel operators — pay a fixed monthly subscription because the data is costly to recreate and they treat it as the industry standard.
  • Subscription-based contracts were 93% of revenue in FY2025, down from 96% in FY2024, and roughly 90% in Q1 2026
  • FY2025 annualized net new bookings were $308 million, up from $250 million in FY2024 and $286 million in FY2023
  • FY2025 selling and marketing was roughly $1,560 million, near 48% of revenue, of which Residential marketing was $771 million versus $77 million for Commercial
capitalization
  • Stock-based compensation rose from $36 million (2016) to $194 million (2025), reaching ~6% of revenue in FY2025, and the diluted share count rose from 324 million to 421 million over the decade — a roughly 30% increase, driven by SBC plus equity issued for M&A (notably 11.7 million shares for Matterport).
competitive-advantage
  • The CoStar information and analytics business holds a durable competitive advantage of the structural kind — a proprietary-data near-monopoly built over 38 years and more than $5 billion of cumulative content spend, defended by the largest research department in the CRE industry and validated by the financial evidence a real moat must leave.
  • contract renewal rate that has held at 89% company-wide for seven straight quarters, with 95% renewal for customers subscribed five years or more and a 92% CoStar quarterly renewal rate
  • stripping those out implies the CoStar suite alone runs a mid-40s percent EBITDA margin
  • Over time the margin tier is stable-to-widening (Commercial Adj EBITDA margin 37.1% in 2023, 39.7% in 2024, 37.6% in 2025, the dip purely Matterport-hardware mix), while the growth premium is eroding: CoStar revenue growth slowed from mid-teens before 2023 to 9% in FY2025 and Q1 2026, even as MSCI and ICE data segments still compound low-double-digits.
  • Apartments.com is a durable franchise — the #1 U.S. multifamily marketplace with 67% brand awareness versus 36% for the #2 competitor, a 99% monthly renewal rate, an NPS near 90, and fifteen consecutive quarters of double-digit revenue growth.
  • The best public proxy for the steady-state economics of a dominant residential portal is Rightmove at a 70% operating margin and REA Group at a 58% EBITDA margin; CoStar's own "core network" (which includes Apartments.com) ran at a 47% margin in Q3 2025, up 400 basis points year-over-year
  • Homes.com has no demonstrated moat yet — it is a marketing-funded attempt to manufacture one, having consumed roughly $3 billion of cumulative investment for only about $106 million of annual run-rate revenue, with management not expecting breakeven until 2030.
  • The one peer datapoint that cuts against Apartments.com on the merits is that Zillow Rentals revenue grew 39% to $630 million while Apartments.com grew roughly 11%; Apartments.com is bigger and more profitable, but its closest competitor is growing rentals revenue faster off a smaller base.
competitive-landscape
  • **CoStar CRE data and analytics** is fragmented by use case rather than dominated by one rival: LightBox owns parcel and geospatial workflow, MSCI/RCA owns transactions and capital markets, Moody's/Reis owns lending forecasts, CompStak owns crowdsourced lease comps, and VTS/Dealpath own workflow.
  • The rising adjacent competitor is CREXi, which is the named LoopNet and Ten-X rival and reported $815.6 billion of active for-sale listings as of November 2025, up 16.7% year-over-year, and more than $1 trillion of cumulative transactions
  • Zillow remains the entrenched #1 with roughly 220 million average monthly users and $514 million of quarterly For Sale revenue, versus Homes.com's $26 million quarterly revenue and $106 million ARR.
  • **Domain and OnTheMarket** give CoStar credible #2/#3 international challengers but not incumbent economics. Domain reaches roughly 6.6 million Australians monthly, about 55% of REA's 12.1 million, but REA's 4.0x visit-frequency advantage implies lower engagement and pricing power
  • Matterport is the category leader in automated 3D capture (1.2 million subscribers, 14.1 million spaces, 50.7 billion square feet) against a fragmented set of cheaper competitors (Zillow 3D Home, CubiCasa, iGuide, CloudPano), but it has no measured unit share and contributed a $146 million net loss in its first ten months under CoStar.
  • The widely cited "$24 billion digital-twin market growing ~41% to $400 billion-plus" TAM is dominated by industrial, manufacturing, and automotive predictive-maintenance use cases, with real estate a minor slice, so any framing that maps Matterport onto that TAM overstates the relevant opportunity by one to two orders of magnitude
  • Ten-X is the leader of a tiny, shrinking niche: online auction was about 1% of CRE transactions when CoStar paid $190 million in 2020, Ten-X had roughly 90% of digital CRE auctions on $24 billion of cumulative sales, but auction revenue has declined for several years to roughly $33 million implied
expectations
  • Residential Adjusted EBITDA improved sharply from −$85 million (Q1 2025) to −$29 million (Q1 2026) as Homes.com marketing rolled off, with management guiding the segment to breakeven-to-$10 million in Q2 2026. The improvement comes from the ~$300 million step-down in 2026 Homes.com net investment.
  • Apartments.com decelerated to 10% in Q1 2026 from 11% in FY2025 and the rooftop windfall that supported 2025 was a one-time event, so the durable Apartments.com run-rate may be high-single-digit, below the FY print.
  • The 15% revenue CAGR to 2028 requires organic acceleration above the recent 11-13% trend or further un-underwritten M&A; management itself concedes the leg may miss.
  • The $1.25 billion 2028 Adjusted EBITDA implies a ~25% margin (up from ~21% guided for 2026) and depends on the unproven "rationalize costs" lever from a management that has historically chosen to invest aggressively.
financial-history
  • From FY2023, the residential build inverted the reported economics: operating income declined from $282 million (2023) to a $72 million loss (2025), net income collapsed to $7 million, and EPS went from $0.92 to $0.02, entirely on Residential marketing (segment marketing rose to $771 million) and the Homes.com monetization launch.
m-and-a
  • CoStar is a disciplined serial acquirer in its older, now-proven core, and an as-yet-unproven one in its newest, largest bets. The judgment rests on the evidence: the apartment and LoopNet roll-ups demonstrably increased the size and strategic quality of the business, STR likely deepened the data moat, and the company has recorded no goodwill or intangible impairments across 2023-2025.
  • The three acquisitions in 14 months lowered the subscription mix and added ~$252 million of acquired revenue in FY2025 (~49% of the year's revenue increase), with Matterport contributing a $146 million net loss and Domain a $28 million net loss in their partial years.
management-incentives
  • the 2022-2024 performance plan settled at 172.8% of target — a 200% revenue payout modified down only to 86.4% by a TSR component — even though three-year total shareholder return was negative 1.5%, in the 33rd percentile of the S&P 500.
  • The binding stewardship question is whether enterprise growth reaches the per-share line: revenue compounded ~16% over the decade, but diluted EPS went from $0.26 (2016) to $0.02 (2025) as the residential build consumed earnings
secular-trends
  • The whole thesis is share gain, not market growth: the end-markets (CRE data, U.S. apartment advertising, residential portals) are low-single-digit-growth or cyclically flat, while CoStar has compounded mid-to-high teens for years.
  • Apartments.com grew 11% in FY2025 and 10% in Q1 2026 into a soft rental-search market by taking competitor rooftops — Apartments.com network visits were up 14% year-over-year in January 2026 while Zillow's rental traffic was down 48% and the broader Zillow-Redfin network down 29%
  • generative-AI and "artificial intelligence" mentions in the 10-K jumped from 2 in FY2024 to 18 in FY2025, and the FY2025 filing added an explicit new mechanism — competitors building "AI-powered platforms that provide access to real estate agents, real estate data, and related content without directing users to our marketplaces," and "rapid advancement of generative AI Technologies may reduce the barrier to entry for new competitors."
  • Reported growth held up, but organic growth slowed, and part of what remains now comes from a Homes.com that does not yet make money.
  • Roughly $3 billion of cumulative investment has produced about $106 million of run-rate revenue, with breakeven not promised until 2030 and a ~$550 million net investment in 2026 alone.
  • Management commits to a 15% revenue CAGR to 2028 and 2030, but organic growth ran 11-13% in 2022-2024; the 15%-plus prints came either off a smaller pre-2021 base or, in 2025-26, with acquisitions.
technology
  • A competitor can license MLS data, public records, cloud compute, and LLMs; it cannot quickly recreate 38 years of standardized CRE history, confidential hotel benchmarking data, lender loan data, proprietary images, and a research organization that the industry voluntarily updates.
  • Apartments.com listings with a Matterport generated far higher engagement — management cites 56x more tour requests per listing and renters spending 46% more time — and Homes.com subscribers with a Matterport on a listing renewed at a 37% higher rate
  • 663 homebuilders had signed data-feed agreements covering roughly 75% of U.S. production new-home activity in Q1 2026, plus 110 million residential parcels added to CoStar and hundreds of thousands of brokerages feeding millions of listings, many not on the open internet
value-chain
  • More than 100% of the company's profit pool sits in Commercial Real Estate. Residential's cumulative Adjusted EBITDA loss across FY2023-FY2025 is roughly $635 million, funded by Commercial's profits and the balance sheet.
  • The scarce resource is not data or capacity — CoStar can manufacture data at will through its research department — it is converting traffic into paid agent subscriptions at rising ARPU, and the marketing required to generate that traffic.
  • the first 11,400 members earned $36,400 more in commissions in year one against a $3,400 average subscription cost, an 11x return, which management frames as evidence the product is underpriced.
