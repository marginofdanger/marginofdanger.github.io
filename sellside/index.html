Goldman Sachs says the macro backdrop for US IPO issuance remains constructive, with its IPO Barometer at 136 and a forecast for record 2026 proceeds of $160 billion, though it has reduced its IPO count forecast to 100 from 120 due to volatility, geopolitics, and software backlog risk. The note highlights mixed post-IPO aftermarket performance, stronger outcomes for fast-growing cos with a near-term path to profitability, typical pre-lockup weakness followed by recovery, and limited evidence that very large upcoming IPOs would create major selling pressure on the broader market or benchmark constituents.

Evidence Quality

No strong hits found.

Important Observations

  • 2026 IPO issuance is running well ahead of last year in both count and proceeds, but sector mix is unusual with Industrials overrepresented and no IT IPOs YTD.
  • Recent IPOs have delivered normal first-day pops but weaker follow-through performance versus history.
  • Best-performing IPOs tend to combine high sales growth with profitability expected within two years.
  • Recent IPOs have shown sharper-than-normal weakness ahead of lockup expiry, but post-lockup rebounds have also been stronger than historical averages.
  • Historical large IPO episodes do not show clear evidence of broad market damage, large-cap selling pressure, or Momentum unwind.
  • Index fast-tracking of mega IPOs may raise inclusion concerns, but initial benchmark weights would still be modest unless float expands materially.

New Data

  • Goldman forecast: roughly 100 US IPOs in 2026 raising $160 billion.
  • Previous forecast was 120 IPOs and $160 billion.
  • 25 IPOs above $25 million have priced YTD, raising $14 billion, nearly 80% above the comparable prior-year period in both number and value.
  • 53 cos have filed YTD, more than double the comparable period last year.
  • GS IPO Barometer is 136 and will now be updated weekly with a higher-frequency CEO confidence input.
  • Average 2026 IPO first-day return is 19%; first three weeks of Q2 averaged 27%.
  • GS Liquid IPO Index (GSX1LIPO) is down 1% YTD versus Russell 2000 up 12%.
  • US equity mutual funds hold 1.4% of assets in cash, or $181 billion, ranked in the 5th percentile of the past 20 years.
  • Hypothetical $1 trillion market cap IPO with 10% float would start below 0.5% weight in S&P 500 and Russell 1000 Growth, and below 2% in Nasdaq-100.

Mgmt Insights

  • Citigroup said IPO markets remain selective, with a flight to quality and more caution in riskier issuance.
  • Wells Fargo said IPO activity slowed late in Q1 but could resume if volatility stabilizes, with a still-active pipeline for the rest of the year.
  • Morgan Stanley said not every co will be able to IPO in the current environment, implying greater issuer selection but potential pipeline resumption if conditions improve.

Risks / Watch Items

  • Geopolitical uncertainty and recent equity market volatility could delay issuance.
  • High software concentration in the IPO backlog remains a headwind.
  • Timing and size of potential mega IPOs create two-sided risk to the 2026 forecast.
  • Post-IPO aftermarket weakness beyond day one remains a concern for new issuance sentiment.
  • Lockup expirations have been a meaningful short-term trading overhang for recent IPOs.
  • Potential benchmark rule changes by index providers could affect inclusion timing and market mechanics.

  • The report is labeled a preliminary draft, so ests and valuation views may still change.
