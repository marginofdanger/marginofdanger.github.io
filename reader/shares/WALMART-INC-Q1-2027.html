Simeon Gutman — Morgan StanleyIncremental margins are at the high-single/low-double-digit range. Outside fuel, what dials them up over time, especially if fuel stays elevated?
  • Core drives mix: strong core + share gains → ability to expand higher-margin platforms; eComm +26%, US marketplace ~+50% are the key enablers for advertising/membership momentum.
  • US eComm incrementals: ~12% this quarter — pleased.
  • Speed fuels frequency: 60% of US households now served in ≤30 min; greater engagement → more membership/advertising utility; the ~1/3-of-earnings recurring streams insulate from fuel/macro.
  • Fuel: experienced logistics/merchandising teams have many levers; positioned to weather all environments.
Greg Melich — Evercore ISITraffic acceleration (3% Walmart US, 6% Sam's) — how to sustain it? And will tariff rebates fund momentum vs. offset energy costs?
  • Traffic drivers: starts with EDLP merchandising; strong GM quarter; fashion + beauty standouts; eComm now 9th straight US quarter of >20% growth; 7,000+ rollbacks delivered in as little as 30 min.
  • Tariff refunds: pursuing the process; best return on $1 of capital right now is price investment for customers — will keep leaning in.
Kate McShane — Goldman SachsWhen do the alternative-revenue platforms launching in Canada & Mexico contribute to enterprise margin?
  • Build once, scale globally: US-built platforms (advertising, membership, data ventures) transferring quickly across North America.
  • International signals (Nicholas): eComm +27%; 30% eComm penetration, 30% membership growth, 30% ads growth — small but fast; inspiration also flows back to US core.
Michael Lasser — UBSShould outsiders temper expectations for consistent double-digit OI growth given recurring external shocks? Where are the alt-revenue streams vs. potential?
  • No tamping needed: Investor Day framework was ~4% top line, ~4–8% OI growth; incrementals can reach double digits (US eComm proved it this quarter).
  • Shocks are exogenous: tariffs last yr, fuel this yr — hence the range; still came in upper half of guide on CC basis.
  • Conviction: marketplace, advertising & fulfillment services each had their best quarter "since I've been here"; not bulletproof, but managing for the long term, not one quarter.
Chris Nardone — BofASustainability of GM strength and ticket assumptions into H2? How does marketplace expansion tie to GM share gains?
  • GM (Rainey): tax refunds higher than expected — some macro lift, won't ascribe all to it; team execution real — fashion outsized, with derivative lift to home decor & beauty (75% of beauty growth from new brands like La Roche-Posay).
  • Marketplace (Dallaire): built on speed-of-delivery rails; benefits all categories; more assortment + faster delivery → visit/purchase frequency → membership + advertising; 3P marketplace ad revenues +50% YoY.
Chris Horvers — JPMorganConsumer behavior shift as tax-refund tailwind fades and fuel squeezes the low end? And does grocery price competition / egg-deflation lapping mute inflation and pressure margins?
  • Bifurcated consumer: high-income spending with confidence; low-income budget-conscious / in distress. Fuel gallons per fill-up fell below 10 for first time since 2022 — a stress signal.
  • Inflation: like-for-like ~+1% in Q1; food fertilizer (nitrogen/phosphates) exposed to Strait of Hormuz closure — possible upward AUR pressure if fuel persists.
  • Eggs: egg deflation cut ~100 bps from the like-for-like number; lapping high-egg periods adds upward pressure to printed YoY inflation later in year.
Paul Lejuez — CitiAre competitors' price investments showing up in US competitiveness? Where do your price gaps sit and how willing to use them? Tariff rate assumptions?
  • Pricing (Furner): 33rd year — pricing always top of mind in food; focused on best value + omnichannel experience; standout = 7,000+ rollbacks live vs. prior 5,000–5,500 run-rate — a meaningful acceleration; EDLP builds trust.
  • Tariffs & fuel (Rainey): generally assuming the current environment.
Oliver Chen — TD CowenWhat's driving the Sparky AOV lift, and what are the key AI-native priorities across customer experience and supply chain?
  • Sparky expansion: live across app, web, in-store; added personalized replenishment, meal planning, smarter recs from inventory/price/speed data.
  • Mission shift: early use was GM discovery; now increasingly everyday essentials (food & consumables) — units purchased via Sparky grew >4x QoQ.
Bob Ciccarelli — TruistTotal usage of fee-generating 30/60-min delivery and the rollout pace of the 30-min-to-60%-of-US capability?

  • Enabler (Furner): 11,000 locations with local inventory + AI-powered data + supply-chain automation (~halfway done) coming online much faster than 2 yrs ago.
