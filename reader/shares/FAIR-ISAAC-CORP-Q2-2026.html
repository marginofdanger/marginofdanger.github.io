Q&A

Jason Haas — Wells Fargo Philosophy behind the $0.99 upfront pricing reset?
  • Multi-year direction: historical upfront-only model concentrates cost on first purchaser; performance model spreads monetization across the value chain to beneficiaries that weren't paying.
  • 10T adoption catalyst: $0.99 + $65 funding fee designed to encourage widespread 10T use — already established in non-conforming, this drives conforming.
What % of lenders shift fully to Vantage vs pulling both?
  • Parity argument: 10T at $0.99 = parity with Vantage on price; 10T wins on predictiveness — no good reason to switch.
  • Gaming scenario: if FHFA doesn't solve gaming and consumers shop rates, originators likely pull both scores — share loss but not volume loss.
Manav Patnaik — Barclays 10T direct license go-live timing & lender feedback?
  • Reseller status: 3 of top 5 signed, deep discussions with remaining 2 — fully expects all 5.
  • Pent-up demand: significant lender interest in performance-based model.
  • Final hurdle: FHFA sign-off on resellers calculating score — math/methodology identical to bureau, no anticipated issues. No firm date but "closing in."
10T historical data release timing — another pilot before 2027?
  • FHFA/GSEs control release timing; market sentiment wants 10T & Vantage evaluated simultaneously — pressure exists, no timeline.
Simon Clinch — Redburn Atlantic How will lenders treat the two pricing options at parity?
  • Lender choice: per-score legacy or $0.99 + funding fee — FICO is "largely indifferent," roughly revenue-neutral.
  • Performance model rationale: low upfront supports widespread prospecting/qualification, aligning with policy goal of more housing/mortgages.
Why is reseller readiness taking longer than expected?
  • Initial expectations "a little optimistic" — multiple moving parts requiring validation/testing; awaiting FHFA sign-off on reseller score calculation is the final gate.
Surinder Thind — Jefferies Sequence of dependencies before FHFA releases historical 10T data?
  • No hidden dependencies — release 10T data, GSEs accept 10T. That's it.
Expense outlook beyond FICO World step-up?
  • Not material overall — incremental personnel, FICO World, marketing; software growth carries COGS, not 100% margin. Backable from guidance numbers.
Faiza Alwy — Deutsche Bank Drivers behind +127% mortgage rev growth — beyond pricing?
  • Decent volume quarter — brief rate dip drove uptick; consistent with bureau commentary; better than guide assumed but they continue to guide conservatively.
Software bookings/platform ARR drivers — verticals beyond financial services?
  • Financial services-driven: not a vertical expansion story — wide range of use cases within FS; platform becomes "heart and soul" of customer interaction, then expands. Land & expand working; high satisfaction.
Jeff Meuler — Baird Can underwriters pull multiple scores earlier in process even if GSEs only accept one?
  • Awaiting GSE selling guidelines — can't speak for them.
What went into approval of the 21 lenders for Vantage 4.0 pilot?
  • Lacking detail — not invited to participate; understanding is "fairly manual process."
Ashish Sabadra — RBC Mid-term pricing strategy — gap between price and value? % of loan amount?
  • Don't shock the market: always evaluating, but no precipitous changes — current moves are first steps in multi-year direction; case for low upfront + monetization spread across value chain remains the philosophy.
Vantage LLPA grids — what credit risks are GSEs accounting for?
  • Risk differential is real: Vantage default risk & prepayment risk differ from FICO.
  • Cycle data gap: Vantage data only goes back to 2013 — never tested through full cycle; downstream investors will demand premium for unknown risk.
  • Pricing dispersion → gaming headache: sometimes Vantage will be better for consumer, sometimes FICO — creates a real problem for the GSEs to solve.
Keen Fai Tong — Goldman Sachs Other implementation hurdles for direct licensing? Why are 2 resellers slower?
  • Just FHFA approval pending — no other meaningful factors. "Very close" on remaining 2.
Guidance assumptions on Vantage adoption / direct licensing timing / performance fee?
  • No volume loss to Vantage assumed in FY26.
  • Revenue neutral between per-score and performance models — minor timing diff (funding fee trails initial fee).
  • Lag built into guide: some performance-model revenue pushed from late FY26 to early FY27.
Alex Hess — JPMorgan Anything beyond rack rate + volume in the +127% mortgage growth?
  • Some customers were on lighter rate last year, now at full wrap rate — primarily new rate + additional volumes. Nothing unusual.
Investments in scores innovation / Ultra FICO?
  • Incremental expense not large; constant innovation investment continues. Ultra FICO likely a significant scores business factor — full plan at FICO World next month.
Kyle Peterson — Needham Will platform-up / non-platform-down trend continue?
  • Two paths: (1) new platform sales (often existing customers, new use cases); (2) legacy → platform migration.
  • Not forcing migration: legacy renewals are highly profitable; customer-led decision. Currently in balance — may shift in future.
Auto originations deceleration — comps, pricing, or volume?
  • Tough comps primary driver; '26 price increases consistent with '25; mix shift toward lower unit-cost pricing tiers gaining volume from higher tiers.
Craig Huber — Huber Research Vantage market share in cards / autos / PL / non-conforming mortgage?
  • Vantage market share ~2% — bureaus give Vantage scores away free with FICO purchases, so reported volumes are largely unpaid.
  • Non-conforming mortgage: lenders use FICO Classic + FICO 10T — they don't use Vantage.
  • Audit gap: FICO numbers are audited; Vantage's are not — share loss would show up in FICO's reported results, and it doesn't.
Why drop upfront fee from $5 to $0.99?
  • (1) Price-competitive with Vantage at low entry point; (2) Encourage 10T adoption — classic new-product launch pricing.
No worry about meaningful Vantage share loss on conforming?
  • Correct — competitive on price, far more competitive on predictiveness; no good reason for share to move.
Ryan Griffin — BMO Securitization market feedback on Vantage?
  • Not ready for Vantage: ~20 mortgages securitized with Vantage paper (<1% of recent securitizations) — market still all FICO; hurdles to overcome.
Expectations for FHFA's summer 10T data release / predictiveness validation?
  • FICO has provided data, ready to go — no timeline. White papers on website analyze 10T vs Vantage on default + prepayment risk; 10T qualifies 5% more borrowers. Encourage looking at third-party analyses coming over weeks/months.
Owen Lau — Clear Street Why can't AI / third-party platforms build a more predictive, cheaper score?
  • Fair lending regulatory wall: AI black-box can't demonstrate non-discrimination — e.g., AI can find 100 proxies for redlining; regulators won't accept opaque underwriting models.
  • Reason codes: FICO ships ~32 reason codes — adverse action letters require explainability; CFPB shut down Upstart's AI underwriting experiment as cautionary tale.
  • Predictiveness leadership: 10T more predictive than Vantage and any other score; only exception = lender-built proprietary scores layered on top of FICO using first-party data.
  • Future-ready: FICO holds patents in explainability + ethical AI — advantaged when AI eventually fits underwriting.
Any of the 21 pilot lenders received updated LLPA grid? When public?
  • No info — heard nothing. "Manual process." Encourages analysts to keep asking.
Scott Wurtzel — Wolfe Research Volume assumption changes since last quarter?
  • Q2 volumes better than guide assumed but not extrapolating — consistently conservative for back half. Hard to make up missed scores rev elsewhere.
Buyback aggressiveness at current levels?
  • Always interested, in market consistently — not market timers but lean in opportunistically. "Current levels — opportunistic."
Kevin McVeigh — UBS Regulatory environment — anything else to watch?
  • $13T mortgage market — no surprise plays: changes telegraphed well in advance; not expecting to be blindsided.
  • GSE privatization upside: if GSEs go private or lose guarantee, emphasis on credit default risk rises — advantage FICO (best score for default prediction).
Curtis Nagle — BofA 10T uptake in non-conforming?
  • ~$1.2T running 10T in parallel with Classic (lenders want latest score available).
Sean Kennedy — Mizuho Adverse selection / gaming dynamics with two-score system?
  • Structural gaming risk: in a two-score world, one will always be more favorable to a given consumer — pulling both is almost inevitable absent FHFA intervention.
  • Share loss ≠ volume loss: if both pulled, market expands by the second score; Vantage could gain "share" via gaming but FICO sees no volume loss.
Auto / card volume weakness late in quarter? Consumer signs?
  • Auto: stable absent economic disruption.
  • Card: bank-marketing-driven; varies QoQ.
  • Consumer: slight subprime falloff offset by prime/super-prime — no significant weakness.
